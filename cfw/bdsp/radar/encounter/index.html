PokéRadar Encounter RNG

Learn how to manipulate PokéRadar encounters to control the internal details of the encountered Pokémon.

As mentioned earlier in the guide, manipulating the PokéRadar encounter after reaching a high chain will grant an IV bonus. We can use IV bonuses to our advantage alongside a high chain length to guarantee that we capture a high IV, shiny Pokémon.

Important - From here it will be assumed that you have calibrated your delay, if you have not done so, go back and do this before continuing. It is also assumed that you have already manipulated your chain length to a number you are comfortable with.
Didn't calibrate your delay? Click here to go back! Want to manipulate chain length? Click here to go back!

Figure 1 - Shiny Patch

Getting Started

Before doing anything else you will need to decide if you want your target Pokémon to be shiny. If you do want the Pokémon to be shiny, the best way to do this is by recharging the PokéRadar until a shiny patch appears. This can be done by doing the following:

Step-by-step
  1. Stand in the center of the patch of grass you are hunting in and use the PokéRadar, check for a shiny patch.
  2. If no shiny patch is produced, walk 50 steps to recharge the PokéRadar and use it again. Avoid these actions:
    • Walking into a patch of shaking grass.
    • Encountering a random wild Pokémon (tip: use Repels).
  3. Repeat until a shiny patch appears, then use the D-Pad to walk towards it and stand directly next to it in any direction.
  4. Press the HOME button to enter the Switch Home Menu, then click Update Seeds on ARSE. This will freeze the RNG state.

If for whatever reason you do not care about the shiny status of your Pokémon you can simply use your D-Pad to walk towards any shaking grass patch and stand directly next to it in any direction instead.


Figure 2 - ARSE Setup

Getting Started

You will now need to feed the program some information about your current in-game status, including the type of grass patch you are targeting, chain length, location, species, and search filters.

Step-by-step
  1. In the General Criteria section, set the Patch: to match the grass patch you are looking at in-game.
  2. Read your chain count from RAM, or manually set your current chain length inside the Chain Count field. By default this is set to 99.
  3. Set the Location to match your current in-game location. If you do not know your current location you can use the map to find out.
  4. Set the species to the one you are currently hunting, this can also be read from RAM using the Read Species from RAM button.
  5. Set your Lead in the dropdown if you are using a Pokémon with the ability Syncronize to control nature.
  6. Now move over to the Search Filters section and enter your search criteria, in this example we are searching for any 6IV.
    • You do not need to define shiny here if you are manipulating a shiny patch, simply leave this section on Ignore.
    • Rare EC should only be checked if you are hunting Dunsparce and want a three-segment Dudunsparce in Scarlet/Violet.
    • You do not need to define height unless you are specifically searching for a Jumbo Marked Pokémon in Scarlet/Violet.
  7. Set the Min. Cluster to a value you are comfortable with. It is recommended to set this value as high as is reasonable.
    • On a chain of 99 it is very easy to find clusters of 10 or higher. The higher the cluster, the better the chance of hitting a target frame.
    • On lower chain lengths, you may wish to set this to a lower value or increase your search range to find results.
    • If you are hunting for a rare EC Dunsparce, consider lowering the cluster size to 2-3 AND increasing your search range.
    • Search range can be increased by adjusting the + field at the bottom of the General Criteria section.
  8. Enter your delay into the Delay field, this should generally be the lowest number or most consistent number from your delay table.

Figure 3 - Target Found

Advancing The RNG State

We found a cluster of x advances that will generate a 6IV Gastrodon on advance x-y. In any case we should aim for the very start of the cluster to grant the best possible chance of hitting the target, so in this example we will aim for advance z.

Step-by-step
  1. Make sure you are connected to ARSE with the Switch Home Menu open on your console, search for your target frame again if necessary.
  2. Advance the RNG state you are around ~1000 advances away from your target, the method you will use to advance the RNG state will vary depending on how far away from the target you are.
    • If you are under 10,000 advances away from your target you can simply let the RNG state advance naturally by standing in the overworld.
    • If you are over 10,000 advances away from the target, it may be in your best interest to use Pokédex scrolling to advance the RNG state quickly.
    • If your target is in the millions of advances away from your current seed, consider using SysBot.NET - Moarencounterbots to automate the process of advancing the RNG state using Dexflip.
    • The usage information for Dexflip can be found on the official Moarencounterbots wiki, or in the general BDSP RNG guide.
  3. Once you are around 1000 advances away from your target you should go back to the Switch Home Menu, click the Update Seeds button on ARSE, and search for your target again.
  4. Slowly inch towards your target frame by quickly pressing the HOME button twice, this will quickly unpause the game and pause it again.
    • Keep a close eye on the Adv. field of ARSE to avoid overshooting your target (note: you may overshoot your target due to bad luck, see below).
  5. Once you have landed on your target frame, keep the game paused on the Switch Home Menu.

In some cases it will not be possible to land directly onto your target frame due to the speed that the RNG state is advancing, this should be treated as a failure. You should find a new target frame and repeat this process again.


Figure 4 - Target Frame Hit

We were able to land directly onto our target frame (x advance), now it is time to initiate the encounter. Hold the correct direction on your control stick (e.g if you are on the left of the shiny patch, hold right on your control stick), then while holding the direction, press the HOME button. This will initiate the encounter.

Figure 5 - Initiating The Encounter

We have now initiated the encounter, and can use the Read Encounter button on ARSE to confirm the internal details match what we expect. The details show that we are in battle with a 6IV, shiny Gastrodon so we have successfully hit the target frame!
