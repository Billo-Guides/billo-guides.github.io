PokéRadar Tips

Understand the basic mechanics of the PokéRadar and how things work under the hood before diving into the actual manipulation process.

The primary use case for manipulating the PokéRadar is to find high IV, shiny Pokémon by building up a high chain. It is important to understand how chains work in order to give yourself the best possible chance of keeping an active chain alive.

Figure 1 - About PokéRadar Chains

About PokéRadar Chains

After using the PokéRadar in a large grass patch, four of the surrounding patches will begin rustling around the player. Encountering any of the Pokémon found inside the rustling grass will begin a chain of the encountered species. When the battle ends the game decides the likelihood the chain continuing based on how you decided to play.

What is considered "optimal play"?
  1. Starting a chain using a grass patch that is 4 tiles away from the player in any direction (including diagonal).
  2. Capturing all Pokémon encountered during your chain, you will want to stock up on strong Poké Balls because of this.
  3. Avoiding doing any of the following, as they are guaranteed to end your chain:
    • Encountering a Pokémon through a normal wild encounter.
    • Running from a Pokémon encountered using the PokéRadar.
    • Mounting your bike while the PokéRadar music is active.
    • Leaving the area by entering any loading zone.
  4. Continuing to encounter Pokémon in grass patches that are 4 tiles away from the player in any direction.
    • If you find that no grass patch within four tiles has spawned, recharge the PokéRadar by taking 50 steps and use it again. Repeat until one appears.

Following these rules will grant a 93% chance of the chain continuing once the battle ends. This means that even with optimal play there is still a 7% chance the chain will randomly break, but this can be controlled with RNG manipulation!

Radar Bonuses

After reaching specific chain lengths the game grants boosts to shiny rates and the number of guaranteed IVs the next encountered Pokémon will have. Detailed information about shiny rates boosts can be found on Serebii's BDSP PokeRadar page, but most notably...

  • At a chain of 19, the next encountered Pokémon will have 1 guaranteed max IV.
  • At a chain of 29, the next encountered Pokémon will have 2 guaranteed max IVs.
  • At a chain of 39, the next encountered Pokémon will have 3 guaranteed max IVs.
  • At a chain of 99, the next encountered Pokémon will have 5 guaranteed max IVs.
  • When a chain reaches 39, the chances of finding a shiny Pokémon are boosted to 1/200.
  • Once a chain reaches 40 or above, the chances of finding a shiny Pokémon caps out at 1/99.

Keep this information in mind going forward as it may influence how you decide to play. For example, you may decide to reach a chain of 39 and continually recharge the PokéRadar until you find a shiny patch, as this will also grant the encountered shiny Pokémon a boost of 3 guaranteed max IVs.

In-Game Setup

You will need to do some initial prep work before getting started with the RNG. The setup required for a seemless experience (particularly with chain length manipulation) might seem oddly specific, but try to follow this information closely.

It may be difficult to gather all of the ideal items and Pokémon required to optimize the RNG experience, but since everyone following this guide should have Switch Custom Firmware you may wish to consider editing your save file to meet the requirements below using PKHeX (instructions on how to edit your save file will not be covered in this guide).

Bag Setup
  1. Have plenty of strong Poké Balls, ideally Master Balls, to be able to capture Pokémon from Radar encounters easily. You will need at least as many as the length of the chain you are trying to achieve.
  2. Have plenty of Repels, ideally Max Repels, to avoid unintentionally encountering a random Wild Pokémon.
  3. PokéRadar and Pokétch app acquired, these can be obtained from Professor Oak at Ramanas Park after completing the main story.
  4. Have the PokéRadar as your only registered item to avoid unintentionally performing actions like mounting the bike (optional but highly recommended).
Party Setup
  1. A full team of six Pokémon to avoid having caught Pokémon go straight into your party as this can cause issues.
  2. Five team members at level 100, with a fainted or level 90+ Pokémon as the final team member. The goal here is to avoid the level up text at the end of a battle.
    • Ideally, your lead Pokémon should be a level 100 Zubat. Using a Zubat makes the RNG advance more slowly than with most other Pokémon due to it not having a blinking animation (it has no eyes!).
    • The lead Pokémon can be switched to a Pokémon with the ability Synchronize when it is time to manipuate the encounter instead of the chain.
  3. A high level Pokémon with the ability Synchronize that is a higher level than the Pokémon on the route to avoid triggering an unwanted encounter when Repel is active.
