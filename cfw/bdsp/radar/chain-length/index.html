PokéRadar Chain Length RNG

Learn how to manipulate PokéRadar chain length for the best possible shiny rates and IV bonuses.

As mentioned earlier in the guide, manipulating your chain length will grant better shiny patch rates and IV bonuses. This section of the guide will teach you how to build a high chain using the automation features found in ARSE.

Getting Started

At the end of a battle, shortly after dismissing the EXP gain screen, the game rolls a random number from 0-99 to decide if the chain will break or continue, with perfect play we want the game to roll a number < 93 for the chain to continue. Remember, there is still a 7% chance that the chain will break even when playing perfectly by encountering a Pokémon four tiles away from the player and capturing it.

Having a Pokémon in our party that can gain EXP is crucial as this gives the player the most control over when the battle ends. The EXP gain screen is dismissed by pressing the A button, which can be automated using ARSE. In practice, this means the tool will automatically press the A button at a point where it is safe to exit the battle without risking the chain.

Because RNG frames advance very quickly during battle, it can be difficult to manually land on a single precise frame that will preserve the chain. For this reason, it is recommended to target ranges that contain 70-100 consecutive safe advances, this provides a buffer and significantly increasing the chance of continuing a chain when the battle ends.

Advances can be done outside of battle using methods such as Pokédex scrolling, then you can enter the battle when you are roughly ~5000 frames away from the safe frame. It is recommended to use this method and target seeds which provide 100 safe frames.

Automating Chain Continuation

It is recommended to do follow these steps using handheld-mode with Joy-Cons properly attached to the console. This will circumvent the need to constantly disconnect and reconnect your controllers on every attempt.

Step-by-step
  1. Use the PokéRadar and stand next to a grass patch that is 4 tiles away from the position the player is standing in and press the HOME button on your controller.
    • Zubat should be the first Pokémon in your party at this point for consistency reasons that were explained earlier in the guide.
  2. Connect to ARSE if you have not already done so. If done correctly you should now see your seed information leftmost column on the main window.
    • If you have already connected to ARSE prior to pressing the HOME button, press the Update Seeds button on ARSE instead.
  3. Set the Min. Safe Advances value to 100 (you can use a lower value if you wish), and the Action at Target to Press A.
    • If you have decided to use a lower value between 70-90, consider entering the battle first as it should not be difficult to find a safe advance.
    • Finding a seed with 70 safe advances will take an average of ~3000 frames, which only takes a few minutes to advance to in battle.
    • Finding a seed with 100 safe advances will take an average of ~40000 frames, which can take a while to advance to in battle.
  4. Keep the Continuation Rate at 93% as you should be playing perfectly to grant yourself the best possible chance of chain continuation.
  5. Click the Generate button towards the bottom of the column, you should now see your Next safe advance at the very bottom of the column.
    • If this value is >5000 and you are not in battle, you can use Pokédex scrolling to quickly adavance the RNG state until you are roughly 5000 advances away from the safe advance.
  6. When you are close to the safe advance, enter the battle and capture the Pokémon, then press the A button until you reach the EXP gain screen.
    • If you are playing in docked-mode you will need to completely disconnect any controllers that are connected to the console at this point, then disconnect and reconnect to the program.
  7. Press the Find Safe Advance button on ARSE, then wait for the program to automatically dismiss the EXP gain screen.
    • You can observe the Status in the leftmost column underneath the Connect / Disconnect buttons to check progress.
  8. If done correctly you should now see that your chain has continued, repeat these steps until you have built your chain to a sufficent length.

Important - While it can be tempting to go for random shiny patches while you are building your chain, it is often not worth the risk of lowering your Continue Rate by going for one that is not at least 4 tiles away.
