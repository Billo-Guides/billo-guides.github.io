PokéRadar Delay Calibration

Learn how to calibrate your area-specific delay for PokéRadar encounters here.

This page outlines the most commonly used method for calculating delay and explains how to use that information to your advantage. Calculating your delay is very important for controlling the stats of PokéRadar encounters, which you will likely want to do after building up your chain. The term "delay" refers to the difference between the RNG advance at the moment of the final input before initiating an encounter, and the advance that generates the Pokémon.

Example - If you initiate an encounter on advance 100, but the Pokémon you battle was generated on advance 180, your delay would be 80. As there is an 80 advance difference between 100 and 180.

Delay Calibration Methods

There are two methods you can use to calibrate your delay, depending on whether you are playing in handheld or docked mode. The method shown above demonstrates the docked-mode approach, however, both methods have their own advantages. You can choose your preferred option by following the written steps outlined below.

Initial Setup
  1. Stand one tile away from the target PokéRadar grass patch. Use the D-Pad to line yourself up precisely with the tile grid for consistent positioning.
  2. Press the HOME button to open the Switch Home Menu. This will freeze the current RNG state.
  3. If you have not already done so, connect your console to ARSE. Your current seed should now appear in the top-left of the window.
  4. If your console was already connected you can simply click the Update Seeds button in ARSE for the same effect.
Docked-Mode Specifics
  1. With your console on the Switch Home Menu, hold the direction on your control stick that will cause the player to directly walk into the PokéRadar grass patch.
    • In the above example we are standing one tile to the left of the grass patch, so we will hold right on the control stick at this stage.
  2. While holding the direction on your control stick, press the HOME button again to resume the game. You should now see that the encounter has started.
  3. Press the Read Encounter button on ARSE to display the internal details of the Pokémon you are in battle with.
  4. Click the Calibate Delay (Docked) button on the right hand-side of the ARSE window. If done correctly you should recieve a pop-up with your calibrated delay.
  5. Repeat this process a few times (5-10 times is recommended) until you have a rough average delay. You can take notes of your delay in a table if you wish.
Handheld-Mode Specifics
  1. With your console on the Switch Home Menu, hold the direction on your control stick that will cause the player to directly walk into the PokéRadar grass patch.
    • In the above example we are standing one tile to the left of the grass patch, so we will hold right on the control stick at this stage.
  2. While holding the direction on your control stick, press the Calibrate Delay (Handheld) button on ARSE, this will prompt the program to resume the game.
  3. After the game is resumed, the encounter will be initated and automatically read by ARSE. If done correctly you should recieve a pop-up with your calibrated delay.
  4. Repeat this process a few times (5-10 times is recommended) until you have a rough average delay. You can take notes of your delay in a table if you wish.
