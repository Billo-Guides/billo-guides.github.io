SwSh Fishing Encounter Guide

This page provides information and worked examples for how you can RNG abuse Pokémon from Fishing encounters. The whole process will be covered here, from finding a desirable location to use to achieving the final result.

Fishing encounters are not guaranteed to be the same species every time, much like a Symbol encounter. The Pokémon species is instead based on an encounterslot value of 0-99. Encounterslot values will change with the weather, the weather can potentially change every time the clock hits 00:00.

Getting Started (Fishing Encounter):
  1. Open SWSH_OWRNG_Generator_GUI, then click on the Encounter Lookup button at the top of the window to open the Encounter Lookup tab.

  2. Type the species name into the empty search field to bring up a list of encounters for that specific Pokémon. Select a row that appeals to you by left clicking it.

  3. Confirm that the filters have been set properly inside the Main Window, encounter filters can be found on the left-hand side.

  4. You will also need to set your KO count correctly in order to produce accurate results, this can be found on the Pokédex entry of the Pokémon you are hunting.

  5. Set your search filters inside the middle portion of the Main Window. You can filter for specific Marks, IVs, Shininess, etc.

  6. Fly to the correct location in-game then stand near to the fishing pool that contains your desired Pokemon, close enough to spawn the fishing pool.

  7. Set the correct weather condition using Luxray's Set function if the current weather in-game is incorrect.

  8. Save the game once you are stood in place, then click the Connect button inside the main window, once connected you can begin searching for target frames.
If your results are not desirable, repeat the following:

  1. Disconnect your Switch from the tool.
  2. Restart your game and enter the overworld.
  3. Pause the game, then reconnect your Switch to the tool.
  4. Search again, repeat until the result is desirable.

Important Information:
The KO mechanic & Brilliant Aura

The KO Mechanic provides a boost to the rates of Shiny & Brilliant Aura Pokémon, you can find the correct value by opening the Pokedéx screen of the Pokémon you are hunting. You can find more details about the odds boosts on Bulbapedia.

Brilliant Aura boosts the amount of guaranteed 31 IVs a Pokémon has, only Pokémon from symbol/fishing encounters can have this trait. This makes fishing encounters ideal for those who are hunting for high IV wild Pokémon.

Dex Recommendations & Ability Boosts

Dex recommendations can be found by opening the Pokedex, when a Pokemon is recommended the encounterslot value for that specific Pokemon is boosted.

Abilities like Lightning Rod, Harvest & Storm Drain boost the encounterslot value for Pokemon of specific types.

Both of these boosting methods are NOT accounted for inside SWSH_OWRNG_Generator_GUI. Avoid using Dexrec or ability boosts in case of inaccuracies.

The Process

The goal is to spawn the fishing pool on the calculated target frame displayed by the generator search results. Once a fishing pool has spawned in the overworld the stats and internal details are set in stone.

Advancing Towards the Target!

After setting up your search filters and standing in the correct position in-game, you will need to calculate the amount of NPCs in the area and the base delay of the encounter. Doing both of these things will provide the best possible chances of hitting the correct target frame.

Delay Calculation & NPC Calibration:
Delay Calculation
  1. Aim for a target frame, such as any shiny Pokémon.
  2. Spawn the Pokémon on the desired target frame, then initiate the encounter.
  3. Take note of the frame that you believe the Pokémon was spawned on.
  4. Read the Pokémon you are in battle with using the Read Encounter button.
  5. If this is the Pokémon you expected, your delay will be 0.
  6. Otherwise, perform a filterless search to find the Pokémon you hit.
  7. Subtract the frame the Pokémon was spawned on from the frame you actually hit.
  8. This will be your delay for that particular encounter.
NPC Calibration
  1. Pause the game then disconnect and reconnect to the program, if done correctly, the number inside the Curr. Adv field of the main window should now be 0.
  2. Click the Menu Close Advances Viewer button at the top of the main window to open the tab. Your seeds should be visible in the correct fields automatically.
  3. Close the pause menu then quickly re-open it, note of the number of advancements that have occured from doing so (see Curr. Adv).
  4. Type 0 into the NPCs field and search. Gradually increase by 1 until the number inside the top row of the Jump column matches the number inside the Curr. Adv field.
  5. Close and re-open the pause menu a few times to make sure that the calculated NPC count is correct.

Take note of the calculated delay and NPC count, you will need this information in order to reach the correct target frame later. Do not change position after calculating the number of NPCs, as this is subject to change depending on positioning.

Hitting Your Target Frame:
Step-by-step tutorial:
  • Advance towards your target frame using any of the methods listed inside the table on the main page. Consider date skipping to advance large distances.

  • Once you are close a few thousand frames away from the target, head back to the correct position in-game (see Figure 2).

  • Pause the game, then disconnect and reconnect to the Main Window and press the search button again. This will be the new target frame.

From this point it is recommended to advance towards the target using only menu close prediction & animations. These types of advancements advance in small increments and are easy to keep track of.
  • Open the Menu Close Advances Viewer tab, and type the number of NPCs (calculated earlier) into the NPCs field.

  • Then type your target frame into the + field and search, factor in the delay that was calculated earlier.

E.g - If the calculated delay is 3, and your target frame is frame 1000, your new target frame is now 997. If you are unable to find your target frame in the search results, use animations to advance the RNG state by 1, repeat until your target frame is visible.
  • Open and close the pause menu until closing the menu a single time lands onto your target frame, according to the Menu Close Advances Viewer prediction.

  • Close the pause menu and run around close to the pond to spawn the fishing pool (without letting the stick rest as this will advance the RNG state).

  • Confirm you have hit the expected result by initiating the encounter, then clicking the Read Encounter button when in battle.
If you encountered an unexpected result, this either means you missed your target frame (too slow) or your delay is incorrect. Confirm your delay using the above mentioned method and try again from the beginning.

By using menu close prediction and animations to keep track of inputs, we were able to hit out target frame!
