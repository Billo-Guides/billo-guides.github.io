Symbol Encounter Guide (CFW)

If you made it this far, it must mean you have an interest in RNG abusing Pokémon from symbol encounters! This page will cover the whole process, from finding a desirable location to achieving a final result.

Table of Contents:

Getting Started
  1. Open the GUI and click on the Encounter Lookup button at the top of the main window. This will open the Encounter Lookup tab.

  2. Type the species name of the Pokémon you are hunting into the empty field. This will bring up a list of every possible encounter and has all the information you need for the main window.

  3. Scroll down the list of encounters and click on one of the rows displaying a SYMBOL encounter. This will automatically enter the information into the main window.

  4. Head to the location in-game, change the date until you meet the correct weather condition, then stand near the spawn point of the Pokémon.

  5. Run far enough away to despawn the Pokémon and save.

  6. Press the X button on your controller to pause the game, and click Connect on the main window of the tool.

Tips & Advice:
  • Symbol encounters are not guaranteed to be the same species every time, the Pokémon species is instead based on an encounterslot value of 0-99.

  • Aiming for clustered frames gives you a higher chance of hitting targets. A cluster is a group of frames that produce the same end result.

  • Brilliant Aura Pokémon are guaranteed to be 2IV upon encounter, consider filtering for Brilliant Aura if your goal is a high IV, shiny Pokémon.

  • Having any Pokémon with the ability Syncronize in the first slot of your party will guarantee that the nature of any encountered overworld Pokémon will be the same as your leading Pokémon.

  • Test how fast the RNG state naturally advances in the area of your target Pokémon, consider locations with calm weather to reduce instability.

  • You should knock out any unwanted static spawns if they are blocking your path towards the grass containing your target.

Important Information
The KO mechanic & Brilliant Aura:
This references the Number Battled: screen of the Pokédex and is used to boost the odds of (Shiny) Brilliant Aura Pokémon. You are able to view the total amount of KO'd Pokémon per species in your entire save file.

Find the Pokédex entry of the Pokémon you are hunting and enter the total number of KOs into the KO Count: field of the main window. This number is important in order to get accurate results from the generator.

Brilliant Aura guarantees 2 full 31 IVs. Players who desire high IV wild caught Pokémon may wish to filter for Brilliant Aura.

Other information:
Want to know the odds based on the total amount of KOs? This Bulbapedia article will tell you everything you need to know.

The generator does not currently provide support for mechanics that boost the encounter rates of specific Pokémon. This includes Dexrec boosts or abilities like Storm Drain. Using spawn boosters like these may output incorrect results.

Some Pokémon from Symbol encounters have flee behavior and will despawn quickly after being spawned in the overworld. Encounter these Pokémon as quickly as you can to avoid disaster.

List of fleeing Pokémon:
Butterfree, Vulpix, Chansey, Horsea, Dunsparce, Blissey, Ralts, Whismur, Drilbur, Audino, Trubbish, Zorua, Ferroseed, Inkay, Phantump, Noibat, Wimpod, Skwovet & Silicobra.

With all the required reading out of the way, it's time to get started!

Time to Get to Work!

The goal is to walk towards the the grass/water containing your target encounter on the calculated target frame displayed on the generator. The advancement methods used are listed on the main page of the guide.

In this example we will be aiming for a Shiny Swablu with a mark in Giant's Bed. You can be more specific with the filters if you wish, such as filtering for a specific mark, specific IVs, or Brilliant Aura.

Using Swablu as the example:
  • The Encounter Lookup tab tells us that Swablu has an encounter slot range of 80-94, a level range of 60-65 and is a symbol encounter in Overcast weather. It also tells us Swablu does not hold a random item, and has 7 potential Egg Moves.
  • After clicking the row of our choice, we can see all the information about the encounter listed on the left hand side of the main window.
  • The Pokedex displays a Number Battled: value of 500 for Swablu, this needs to be entered into the KO: Count field of the generator.
  • We are paused in-game, and our current seeds are visible on the main window after clicking Connect.
  • Now all the details of our encounter are set up properly and we are ready to search or targets!

Game Setup
This location in Giant's Bed is ideal, there are no NPCs around and there are no spawned Pokémon present. Giant's Bed is located in within Crown Tundra, which is a notably quiet area in calm weather conditions.

Once a Pokémon is spawned in the grass/water, the stats and internal details (such as PID/EC) are generated and set in stone. This is why it is only important to spawn the Pokémon in the overworld on the correct frame (And to not let a target Pokémon despawn!).

Once the main window is filled in correctly & you are standing in a good position in-game, you can begin looking for target frames by clicking the search button on main window of the generator.

If your results are not desirable, repeat the following:

  1. Disconnect your Switch from the tool.
  2. Restart your game and enter the overworld.
  3. Pause the game, then reconnect your Switch to the tool.
  4. Search again, do this until you have desirable results.

Advancing towards the target!

After pressing search on the main window the results display a shiny Swablu on frame 2007, 2008 & 2009. Based on local testing the delay for this symbol encounter is 3. This means the target for this attempt remains at frame 2004-2007.

How to calculate delays:
  1. Aim for a target, such as any shiny Pokémon.
  2. Run towards the Pokémon and spawn it in the overworld then initiate the encounter. Take note of the frame the Pokémon was spawned on.
  3. Encounter the Pokémon and click the Read Encounter button.
  4. If you didn't find the Pokémon that you expected, do a filterless search (or search for the specific IVs of the result you found) and find the frame you hit.
  5. Subtract your target advancement from the frame you actually hit.
  6. This will be your delay for that particular encounter.

Hitting your target frame
Step-by-step tutorial:

  1. Advance towards your target frame using any of the methods listed inside the table on main page of this guide. Consider using the Skip feature to advance large distances with day skipping.

  2. Once you are within a few hundred frames of your target, stand near to the spawn point of the Pokémon you are hunting.

  3. Pause the game using the X button, then disconnect and reconnect to the main window, then search for your target again.

    4. Tip: From this point it's recommended to only advance using menu close & animations as they only advance in small increments, making them easy to keep track of.

  4. Open the Menu Close Advances Viewer window and work out the amount of NPCs that are in the area, as explained on the main page. Enter this number into the NPCs field.

  5. Enter your target frame into the + field of this window and hit search. This should be the highest number in the cluster.

    6. Tip: If you are unable to find your target frame in the search results, this means the target cannot be hit based on your seed. Use animations to advance the RNG state slightly, then close and re-open the Menu Close Advances Viewer window and search for your target frame again. Repeat this process until your target frame is visible.

  6. Open and close the pause menu until closing the menu a single time lands onto your target frame, according to the Menu Close Advances Viewer prediction. Pay attention to the Curr. Adv field of the main window to avoid overshooting your target.

  7. Close the pause menu and quickly run towards the grass/water to spawn your Pokémon and initate an encounter! Confirm you have hit the expected result by clicking the Read Encounter button when in battle.

    8. You can be creative with the methods you use to advance the RNG state. Menu close prediction is not necessary but will make for a much easier time hitting target frames and is extremely helpful in areas of the game where the RNG advances at a quick pace.
