SwSh Roaming Encounter Guide

This page provides information and worked examples for how you can RNG abuse Galarian Articuno, Zapdos, & Moltres. The whole process will be covered here, from finding a desirable location to use to achieving the final result.

Each bird spawns in one of the Wild Area, Articuno can be found in Crown Tundra, Zapdos can be found in base Wild Area, and Moltres can be found in the Isle of Armor, you must complete the Crown Tundra main quest to spawn these encounters in the overworld.

Getting Started (Symbol Encounter):
  1. Open SWSH_OWRNG_Generator_GUI, then click on the Encounter Lookup button at the top of the window to open the Encounter Lookup tab.

  2. Type the species name of the bird you are hunting, then select a row that has calm weather by left clicking it.

  3. Confirm that the filters have been set properly inside the Main Window, encounter filters can be found on the left-hand side.

  4. Set your search filters inside the middle portion of the Main Window. You can filter for specific Marks, IVs, Shininess, etc.

  5. Fly to the correct location in-game, then set the correct weather condition using Luxray's Set function if the current weather in-game is incorrect.

  6. Save the game once you are stood in place, then click the Connect button inside the main window, once connected you can begin searching for target frames.
If your results are not desirable, repeat the following:

  1. Disconnect your Switch from the tool.
  2. Restart your game and enter the overworld.
  3. Pause the game, then reconnect your Switch to the tool.
  4. Search again, repeat until the result is desirable.

The Process

The goal is to use fly to generate the bird in the Wild Area on the calculated target frame displayed by the generator search results. Once the bird has spawned in the overworld the stats and internal details are set in stone until you leave the area.

From here the it is assumed that your encounter & search filters have been properly configured (see Figure 1). The advancement methods used are throughout this guide are listed inside the table found on the main page.

Advancing Towards the Target!

After setting up your search filters and standing in the correct position in-game, you will need to calculate the base delay of the encounter. Doing this will provide the best possible chances of hitting the correct target frame.

Delay Calculation:
Delay Calculation
  1. Aim for a target frame, such as any marked bird.
  2. Spawn the Pokémon on the desired target frame, then initiate the encounter.
  3. Take note of the frame that you flew into the area on.
  4. Read the Pokémon you are in battle with using the Read Encounter button.
  5. If this is the Pokémon you expected, your delay will be 0.
  6. Otherwise, perform a filterless search to find the Pokémon you hit.
  7. Subtract the frame the Pokémon was spawned on from the frame you actually hit.
  8. This will be your delay for that particular encounter.
Recommended starting delays:
  • Articuno - 130-140
  • Zapdos - 20-30
  • Moltres - 60-80

    • Take note of the calculated delay, you will need this information in order to reach the correct target frame later. Do not change position after calculating the delay, as this is subject to change depending on positioning.

    Hitting Your Target Frame:
    Step-by-step tutorial:
    • Advance towards your target frame using any of the methods listed inside the table on the main page. Consider date skipping to advance large distances.

    • Once you are close a few thousand frames away from the target, head back to the correct position in-game (see Figure 2).

    • Pause the game, then disconnect and reconnect to the Main Window and press the search button again. This will be the new target frame.

    From this point it is recommended to advance towards the target using only menu close prediction & animations. These types of advancements advance in small increments and are easy to keep track of.
    • Open the map, take note of the amount of advancements that occur from doing so, repeat this a few times to get an average number from the results.

    • Open the party menu and advance the remainder of required advancements using animations & menu close, factor in the delay that was calculated earlier.

    E.g - If the calculated delay is 130, and your target frame is frame 1000, the new target frame will be 870. If an average of 2 advancements occur from opening the map, this means you should aim to open the map on frame 868 (1000-130-2).
    • Open the map on the correct target frame, then fly into the area that contains your chosen bird.

    • Initate the encounter! Confirm you have hit the expected result by clicking the Read Encounter button when in battle.
    If you encountered an unexpected result, this either means you missed your target frame (too slow) or your delay is incorrect. Confirm your delay using the above mentioned method and try again from the beginning.

    By using animations and the Curr. Adv. field of the generator to keep track of inputs, we were able to hit out target frame!
