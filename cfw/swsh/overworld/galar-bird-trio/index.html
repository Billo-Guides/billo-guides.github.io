Roaming Encounter Guide (CFW)

This portion of the guide will cover the process of RNG abusing Galarian Articuno, Zapdos, & Moltres. It will cover the whole process, from finding a desirable location to achieving a final result.

Table of Contents:
  • Getting started (This is where you are now)
  • Getting to work
  • Advancing towards the target
Getting Started
  1. Open the GUI and click on the Encounter Lookup button at the top of the main window. This will open the Encounter Lookup tab.

  2. Type the species name of the bird you are hunting into the empty field. This will bring up a list of every possible encounter and has all the information you need for the main window.

  3. Scroll down the list of encounters and click on one of the rows displaying your desired area. This will automatically enter the information into the main window.

  4. Fly to the location in-game and save.

  5. Press the X button on your controller to pause the game, and click Connect on the main window of the tool.

In-game requirements & Tips:
  • Have the legendary birds roaming in each of the Wild Areas by completing the Crown Tundra main quest.

  • Unlock a fly point in each of the areas. Snowslide Slope (Articuno), East Lake Axewell (Zapdos), and Fields of honor (Moltres) are recommended.

  • Aiming for clustered frames gives you a higher chance of hitting targets. A cluster is a group of frames that produce the same end result.

  • Having any Pokémon with the ability Syncronize in the first slot of your party will guarantee that the nature of any encountered overworld Pokémon will be the same as your leading Pokémon.

With all the required reading out of the way, it's time to get started!

Time to Get to Work!

The goal is to fly into the location of our desired bird on the calculated target frame given by the generator. The advancement methods used are listed on the main page of this guide, day skip, menu close and fly advances will be used for this example.

Important Information:
The delay for each of the birds varies. It's recommended to take a few practice attempts and note down your delay for the highest chances of being able to hit the target frame. See the Troubleshooting Encounters section at the bottom of this page if you are running into problems with delay calculation.

The birds will regenerate upon flying into the area. This means you can simply retry if you miss your target frame.

These encounters are shiny locked, so filtering for shiny Pokémon should be avoided here.

Recommended fly point & starting delays:

Articuno
Location: Crown Tundra
Recommended fly point: Snowslide Slope
Recommended starting delay: 130-140

Zapdos
Location: Wild Area (Meetup Point/Motostoke Area)
Recommended fly point: East Lake Axewell
Recommended starting delay: 20-30

Moltres
Location: Isle of Armor
Recommended fly point: Master Dojo
Recommended starting delay: 60-80


We will be aiming for a 5IV marked Zapdos in this example. You can be more specific with the filters if you wish, such as filtering for a specific mark or specific IVs. The process for RNG abusing each of the birds is the same. You will simply fly to the correct location depending on which of the birds you are hunting.

Game Setup
These three fly points are suggested as they're close to the spawn point of each of the birds, meaning we can encounter them more quickly.

Once a roaming bird has spawned in the overworld, the stats and internal details (such as PID/EC) are generated and set in stone. This is why it is only important to spawn the Pokémon in the overworld on the correct frame (And to not let a target Pokémon despawn!).

Now that the main window is filled in correctly & you are standing in a good position in-game, you can begin looking for target frames by clicking the search button on main window of the generator.

If your results are not desirable, repeat the following:

  1. Disconnect your Switch from the tool.
  2. Restart your game and enter the overworld.
  3. Pause the game, then reconnect your Switch to the tool.
  4. Search again, do this until you have desirable results.

Advancing towards the target!

The generator displays the target on frame 580-583, based on local testing the delay for this static encounter is 30. This means the target remains at frame 550-553.

How to calculate delays:
  1. Aim for a target, such as any marked roaming Pokémon.
  2. Fly into an area with a roaming encounter then chase it down to initiate the encounter. Take note of the frame that you flew into the area on.
  3. Encounter the Pokémon and click the Read Encounter button.
  4. If you didn't find the Pokémon that you expected, do a filterless search (or search for the specific IVs of the result you found) and find the frame you hit.
  5. Subtract your target advancement from the frame you actually hit.
  6. This will be your delay for that particular encounter.

Hitting your target frame
Step-by-step tutorial:

  1. Advance towards your target frame using any of the methods listed inside the table on main page of this guide. Consider using the Skip feature to advance large distances with day skipping.

  2. Once you are within a thousand frames of your target, fly into the area of the Pokémon you are hunting.

  3. Pause the game using the X button, then disconnect and reconnect to the main window, then search for your target again.

    4. Tip: From this point it's recommended to only advance using animations as they only advance in small increments, making them easy to keep track of.

  4. Open and close the map, take note of how many advances occur from doing so, repeat this step multiple times and take an average number from the results.

  5. Open the party menu and advance to your target frame using animations, here's where we need to factor in the amount of advances that pass from closing the map.

    6. Example: If your target frame is on advance 100, the generation delay is 30, and the average amount of advancements from opening the town map is 2, we will need to open the town map on advance 68 in order to hit the target frame. (68+2+30=100)

  6. Open the map and fly into the area to spawn your bird! Encounter it, then click the Read Encounter button. Make sure it matches the output shown on the generator.

    7. You can be creative with the methods you use to advance the RNG state. The above method is the recommended method that provides the most consistency of hitting target frames for roaming encounters.

By using animations and the Curr. Adv. field of the generator to keep track of inputs, we were able to hit out target frame!
