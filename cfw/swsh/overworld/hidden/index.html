Hidden Encounter RNG (CFW)

If you made it this far, it must mean you have an interest in RNG abusing Pokémon from hidden encounters! This page will cover the whole process, from finding a desirable location to achieving a final result.

Getting Started:

Before starting, do the following:
  • Open the GUI and click on the Encounter Lookup button at the top of the main window. This will open the Encounter Lookup tab.
  • Type the species name of the Pokémon you are hunting into the empty field, this will bring up a list of every possible encounter and has all the information you need for the main window.
  • Scroll down the list of encounters and click on one of the rows displaying a SYMBOL encounter, this will automatically enter the information into the main window.
  • Head to the location in-game, change the date until you meet the correct weather condition, then stand near the spawn point of the Pokémon.
  • Run far enough away to despawn the Pokémon and save.
  • Press the Home button on your controller, click Connect on the main window of the tool.
It's suggested to pick an encounter in a quiet area with calm weather to reduce instability. Noise is area specific, you should test how noisy an area is before attempting to RNG abuse Pokémon in it.

With all the required reading out of the way, it's time to get started!

Time to get to work!

The goal is to walk into grass and spawn a hidden encounter on the calculated target frame given by the generator. The advancement methods used are listed on the main page of this guide.

We will be aiming for a Shiny Sinistea-Antique in Glimwood Tangle. You can be more specific with the filters if you wish, such as filtering for a specific mark or specific IVs.

Using Sinistea-Antique as the example:
- The Encounter Lookup tab tells us that Sinistea-Antique in Glimwood Tangle has an encounter slot range of 99-99, a level range of 34-36 and is a hidden encounter in all weather. It also tells us Sinistea-Antique does not hold a random item and has no potential Egg Moves.
- Clicking the row that applies to our encounter will enter all the information we need into the main window.
- Number of KOs is irrelevant as hidden encounters cannot be Brilliant Aura.

Let's enter this information into the main window by clicking the row that matches our conditions!

  2. Restart your game and enter the overworld.
  3. Enter the pause or Home menu and reconnect your Switch to the tool.
  4. Search again, do this until you have desirable results.

We found one! A shiny, gentle natured Sinistea-Antique in a cluster on advances 19,139 and 19,141. These are the "target frames" and the aim will be to spawn a hidden encounter in the grass on either of these frames.

Hidden encounters have a chance of spawning per step taken while in grass. If the encounter does not spawn in the first 1-2 steps there is a good chance that you will miss your target frame.

Once a hidden encounter is spawned the stats and internal details (such as PID/EC) are generated and set in stone, this is why it is only important to spawn the encounter on the correct frame.

Don't let the Pokémon despawn after you spawn it in the overworld! Hidden encounters will despawn within a few seconds of appearing, run into the encounter quickly.

It's common to see "clusters" or "groups" of frames that produce the same result when searching for a target. Aiming for large clusters is recommended as this will provide more chances for the player to spawn the Pokémon on the correct frame.

Advancing towards the target:
The generator displays the target on frame 19,139 and 19,141, based on local testing the delay for this symbol encounter is 3. This means the target frames for this attempt will be frame 19,136 and 19,138.

Step by step:
  1. Advance towards the target frame using bike, NPCs or by spawning overworld Pokémon in the area.

    2. Consider using the DaySkip feature of the GUI to advance large distances.

  2. Once you are close to the target (within ~500 frames), stand close to a patch of grass in the area and click the Update States button.

    3. This will update your current seeds at the top right of the window.

    From this point it's recommended to only advance using menu close, animation and bike advancements as they only advance in small increments, making them easy to keep track of.

  3. Quickly close and reopen the pause menu, pay attention to the number of advancements that have passed from doing so (shown in the Curr. Adv. field on the main window), repeat this step roughly 5 times.

    4. It's important to know the average number of frames that will advance from closing the pause menu, we will eventually need to account for this to hit the correct target frame.

    From this point it's recommended to only advance using animations from the party menu, this provide a stable +1 advancement per input.

  4. Open the party menu and advance to your target frame using animations, here's where we need to factor in the amount of advances that pass from closing the pause menu.

    5. For example, if the delay in the area is 3, the average number of frames from closing the pause menu is 8 and the target is 100 advances away, we would advance to frame 89 using animations, then closing the pause menu would pick up the 8 advancements we need to hit the target frame.

  5. Close the pause menu and quickly run into grass to spawn an encounter! If a hidden encounter spawned, great! Encounter it then click the Read Encounter button. Make sure it matches the output shown on the generator.

    6. If a hidden encounter did not spawn you were simply unlucky and will have to look for a new target frame.
You can be creative with the methods you use to advance the RNG state.

Troubleshooting Encounters

This section covers how to troubleshoot encounters and delays. If you miss a target, spawning in the wrong Pokémon, or can't find your result on the RNG tool, this is the place to be.

Missing Targets:

The commonly occuring reasons for missing a target Pokémon are:
  • Encounter did not spawn.
  • Encounter spawned too late due to mistiming.
  • There is a delay left unaccounted for.
  • Frame was "hit" but an incorrect species of Pokémon was encountered.
  • Information in the main window was not completely accurate.
Encounter did not spawn or spawned too late...
This is a common issue players will face when tackling hidden encounters and is likely due to mistiming when spawning the Pokémon. Retry and search for a new target frame and make sure your player positioning is correct.

Standing directly next to the grass is advised as you will be able to spawn a hidden encounter in 1-2 steps. You will know if you are standing too close when the player starts the crouching animation, this is something we want to avoid.

Troubleshooting delay...
For the purposes of this guide, the term "delay" is used to describe the frame difference between the Pokémon we encountered and the frame the encounter was initiated on.

Most hidden encounters need to account for some amount of delay, this applies most to encounters in extreme weather conditions or in an otherwise unstable environment, like having other spawned Pokémon present.

Delay Calc - frame Pokémon was generated - frame encounter was initiated = delay

We can use the Read Encounter feature of the tool to confirm the exact frame the encounter was generated on and calculate the delay from there. Here's how it's done!
  • Aim for a target, such as any shiny Pokémon.
  • Walk towards the grass, spawn the Pokemon and initiate an encounter. Take note of the frame the Pokémon was spawned on.
  • Encounter the Pokémon and click the Read Encounter button.
  • If you didn't find the Pokémon that you expected, do a filterless search (or search for the specific IVs of the result you found) and find the frame you hit.
  • Subtract your target advancement from the frame you actually hit.
  • This will be your delay for that particular encounter.
When RNG abusing in unstable conditions you should record delays for multiple attempts before aiming for a target and check for any consistent delay counts. It is also advised to aim for large clusters where possible to increase your chances of actually hitting the target.

Cannot find result...
If you cannot find the Pokémon you are in battle with anywhere on the tool this is likely due to one of two reasons:
  • The encounter spawned too late and you missed your target frame as a result.
  • The information entered into the main window of the tool was not completely accurate.
Try to reposition your character and see if your issue persists, if you are still having problems double check the information on the main window is entered correctly by searching it on the Encounter Lookup and clicking the correct row.

You must pay attention to the row you are clicking and making sure it matches your in-game environment.
