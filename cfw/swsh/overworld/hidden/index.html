SwSh Hidden Encounter Guide

This page provides information and worked examples for how you can RNG abuse Pokémon from Hidden encounters. The whole process will be covered here, from finding a desirable location to use to achieving the final result.

Hidden encounters are not guaranteed to be the same species every time, the Pokémon species is instead based on an encounterslot value of 0-99. The encounterslot values will change with the weather, the weather can potentially change every time the clock hits 00:00.

Getting Started (Hidden Encounter):
  1. Open SWSH_OWRNG_Generator_GUI, then click on the Encounter Lookup button at the top of the window to open the Encounter Lookup tab.

  2. Type the species name into the empty search field to bring up a list of encounters for that specific Pokémon. Select a row that appeals to you by left clicking it.

  3. Confirm that the the filters have been set properly inside the Main Window, encounter filters can be found on the left-hand side.

  4. Set your search filters inside the middle portion of the Main Window. You can filter for specific Marks, IVs, Shininess, etc.

  5. Fly to the correct location in-game, then set the correct weather condition using Luxray's Set function if the current weather in-game is incorrect.

  6. Stand next to a patch of tall grass, save the game, then click the Connect button inside the main window, once connected you can begin searching for target frames.
If your results are not desirable, repeat the following:

  1. Disconnect your Switch from the tool.
  2. Restart your game and enter the overworld.
  3. Pause the game, then reconnect your Switch to the tool.
  4. Search again, repeat until the result is desirable.

The Process

The goal is to walk into the grass on calculated target frame displayed by the generator search results. Once a hidden has spawned in the overworld the stats and internal details are set in stone, hidden encounters will disappear rapidly so encounter the Pokémon quickly!

From here the it is assumed that your encounter & search filters have been properly configured (see Figure 1). The advancement methods used are throughout this guide are listed inside the table found on the main page.

Game Setup:

For Hidden encounters, the best place to stand would be directly next to a patch of grass in the area. The smaller the patch, the better as this will reduce the amount of spawned overworld Pokemon.

The above location is ideal, there are a low number of NPCs in the area, the grass patch is small, and there are no extra overworld Pokémon present.

If you are hunting for hidden encounters in other areas, you may notice that it is impossible to find a patch of grass with no overworld spawns. This will reduce the effectiveness of closing the pause menu to freeze the RNG state.

If you cannot find a good position to stand in, you can knock out any static spawns that block your path to the Pokémon, it will remain despawned until return to the area after a date has been changed.

Advancing Towards the Target!

After setting up your search filters and standing in the correct position in-game, you will need to calculate the amount of NPCs in the area and the base delay of the encounter. Doing both of these things will provide the best possible chances of hitting the correct target frame.

Delay Calculation & NPC Calibration:
Delay Calculation
  1. Aim for a target frame, such as any shiny Pokémon.
  2. Spawn the Pokémon on the desired target frame, then initiate the encounter.
  3. Take note of the frame that you believe the Pokémon was spawned on.
  4. Read the Pokémon you are in battle with using the Read Encounter button.
  5. If this is the Pokémon you expected, your delay will be 0.
  6. Otherwise, perform a filterless search to find the Pokémon you hit.
  7. Subtract the frame the Pokémon was spawned on from the frame you actually hit.
  8. This will be your delay for that particular encounter.
Hidden encounters have the chance to spawn per step taken in grass, they may not spawn quickly enough which means you will miss your target as a result. Spawn the encounter within 1-3 steps when calculating delay for the most accurate results.
NPC Calibration
  1. Pause the game then disconnect and reconnect to the program, if done correctly, the number inside the Curr. Adv field of the main window should now be 0.
  2. Click the Menu Close Advances Viewer button at the top of the main window to open the tab. Your seeds should be visible in the correct fields automatically.
  3. Close the pause menu then quickly re-open it, note of the number of advancements that have occured from doing so (see Curr. Adv).
  4. Type 0 into the NPCs field and search. Gradually increase by 1 until the number inside the top row of the Jump column matches the number inside the Curr. Adv field.
  5. Close and re-open the pause menu a few times to make sure that the calculated NPC count is correct.

Take note of the calculated delay and NPC count, you will need this information in order to reach the correct target frame later. Do not change position after calculating the number of NPCs, as this is subject to change depending on positioning.

Hitting Your Target Frame:
Step-by-step tutorial:
  • Advance towards your target frame using any of the methods listed inside the table on the main page. Consider date skipping to advance large distances.

  • Once you are close a few thousand frames away from the target, head back to the correct position in-game (see Figure 2).

  • Pause the game, then disconnect and reconnect to the Main Window and press the search button again. This will be the new target frame.

From this point it is recommended to advance towards the target using only menu close prediction & animation advancements. These methods advance frames in small increments, making them easy to keep track of.
  • Open the Menu Close Advances Viewer tab, and type the calculated number of NPCs into the NPCs field.

  • Then type your target frame into the + field and search, factor in the delay that was calculated earlier (Example: 1000 (target) - 3 (delay) = 997 (new target)).

If you are unable to find your target frame in the search results, use animations to advance the RNG state by 1, repeat until your target frame is visible inside the Menu Close Advances Viewer window.

If you cannot hit your target frame using menu close prediction, use it to get as close as you can. Afterwards you can use an alternative advancement method (e.g player movement) to hit your target frame.
  • Open and close the pause menu until closing the menu a single time lands onto your target frame, according to the Menu Close Advances Viewer prediction.

  • Close the pause menu and quickly run into the grass to try to spawn an encounter, then initate the encounter! Confirm you have hit the expected result by clicking the Read Encounter button when in battle.
If you encountered an unexpected result, this either means you missed your target frame (too slow), the encounter did not spawn on time, or your delay is incorrect. Confirm your delay using the above mentioned method and retry.
