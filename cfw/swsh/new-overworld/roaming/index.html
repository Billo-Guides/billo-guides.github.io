Overworld Roaming Encounters

Worked examples for manipulating Articuno, Zapdos and Moltres in any weather condition.

The roaming Galarian bird trio, Articuno, Zapdos, & Moltres can be found in their respective Wild Areas after completing the Crown Tundra DLC - Legendary Quest 3. These Pokémon are generated upon entering a loading zone, and their stat details (IVs/Nature/Mark) remain locked in place until the area is reloaded.

By using a technique known as "fly manip", we can precisely respawn the bird on a specific frame to generate it with the desired IVs, Nature, and Marks. Once the bird respawns, we can chase it down and initiate the encounter.

Important - From here it is assumed that you are familiar with key concepts described on the main page. If you are unsure about topics like seed monitoring, advancement methods, or NPC calibration, please review the relevant sections before continuing.

Figure 1 - RNG Tool Setup

Getting Started (Tool Setup):
  1. Enter the IP address of your console in the main window and click Connect. This will automatically read your current seed and save file details.

  2. Select Static in the Encounter Settings column and select the following Area: based on your desired bird:
    • For Articuno-Galar, select Giant's Bed.
    • For Zapdos-Galar, select East Lake Axewell.
    • For Moltres-Galar, select Challenge Road.
    • After you have selected a location, set your target Pokémon accordingly.

  3. Select your current or desired weather condition, Keep in mind that weather is subject to change if the system time reaches midnight (00:00).

  4. Under Advanced Settings, click the Fly? checkbox. This is where we will consider the necessary values for fly manip. Leave the Area Load & Fly NPCs at 0 for now.

  5. Adjust your search range using the + field in Encounter Settings, then configure additional search filters such as Marks, IVs, or Height.

DO NOT APPLY SHINY SEARCH FILTERS! THESE ENCOUNTERS ARE SHINY LOCKED, FILTERING FOR SHINY VARIANTS WILL NEVER PRODUCE A VALID SEARCH RESULT.


Figure 2 - Game Setup

Game Setup:
  1. You will need to unlock the fly points shown in Figure 2 if you have not already done so, the fly points for Articuno and Moltres will require DLC story progression.
    • For Articuno-Galar, unlock the fly point to the east of Giant's Bed by interacting with the Regi cave close by.
    • For Zapdos-Galar, unlock the fly point below Motostoke, this fly point should be active already through main story progression.
    • For Moltres-Galar, unlock the fly point at the Tower of Darkness, DLC progress will vary depending on which tower was initially picked.

  2. Remove all the Pokémon in your party until you have only one left. This is a crucial step as the RNG tool assumes you have a single Pokémon in your party.
    • It is recommended to use a Pokémon with the Ability "Synchronize" that has the Nature you are aiming for on your desired bird.

  3. Visit the Memory Check NPC found in any PokéCenter and repeatedly rename your remaining Pokémon until it has a memory intensity of "fondly remembers".
    • The simplest way to achieve a "fondly" intensity memory is by renaming your Pokémon multiple times. Confirm the memory intensity by speaking to the NPC.

Box memories are common types of memory that have an intensity of "definitely remembers", which is one step away from being "fondly remembers". For the most convenient and quickest solution, rename a Pokémon that already has a memory intensity of "definitely remembers".

Advancing Towards A Target

Step-by-step instructions for hitting a target frame can be found here.

Figure 4 - Relevant Calibration

Settings Toggles:
  1. Ensure you are on a freshly advanced day - If you have touched anything on the map today or are not sure, perform a date advance. This refreshes the overworld state.

  2. Fly to the required location for your target bird: East Giant's Bed (Articuno), East Lake Axewell (Zapdos), or Challenge Road (Moltres).

  3. Confirm that you have a single Pokémon in your party, this should be the same Pokémon that was given a "fondly" intensity memory earlier.

  4. Set the current weather condition to anything that is NOT Rain/Thunderstorm both in-game and on owoow. Save the game after doing so.

  5. Calibrate your current menu close NPC count, then close the MenuCloseTimeline sub-window. Note down your current NPC count in Notepad or similar.

  6. Set your current menu close NPC count to 0 for now, but ensure that the Consider Menu Close? checkbox remains activated.

  7. Refer to the tables above, ensure that you are looking at the correct table based on your chosen weather, target bird, or game version/progression (Moltres).

    • Set your Area Load value in the Advanced Settings on owoow, this will be the same regardless of weather condition.
    • If you are NOT in Rain/Thunderstorm, enter the Total Fly NPC value into the Fly NPC field in Advanced Settings.
    • If you are in Rain/Thunderstorm, enter the NPC 0 value into the menu close NPC field, then enter the NPC 1 value into the Fly NPC field in Advanced Settings.
    • Enter the Rain Tick value depending on if you are in Rain or Thunderstorm, if this is your first time, pick the lowest possible value (7 for Rain, 14 for Thunder).

The calibration values (Consider Menu Close? / Menu Close NPCs Area Load / Fly NPCs / Rain Ticks) will account for the advances consumed when exiting from the party list -> main pause menu -> opening map -> flying into the area that contains your bird.

After correctly setting your calibration values you can begin searching for targets.

Advancing to the Target

In this example, 6IV Zapdos with the Rare Mark appears in a {% include tooltips/clusters.html %} between advances x-y. The the relevant values we need were calibrated earlier, and for this case, no direction should be held when closing the pause menu to maximize the chances of hitting the correct frame.

Step-by-Step:
  1. Advance toward your target using either wet weather, npc activation or date skipping. Consider using wet weather instead if you wish to maintain calibration.

  2. Stop advancing once you are a few thousand advances before your target, then fly back into the area that contains your desired bird if you have moved.

  3. If date skipping was used to advance the RNG state, you will need to re-calibrate your current menu close NPC count and update the calibration checkboxes accordingly.

  4. Ensure that the Consider Menu Close? checkbox is checked in the main window, and confirm that your calibration checkboxes are filled in correctly.

  5. Pause the game, then use animations to advance the RNG state until you are approximately 100 advances away from your target.

  6. Keep the game open on the Pokémon party list from this point. Reconnect to owoow and search for your target again, then use animations to reach your target frame.

  7. Back out to the main pause menu and open the map, then fly into the required location for your desired bird.

  8. Chase down the bird and encounter it to verify that your result matches what is expected. If your bird does NOT match, refer to the troubleshooting section below.
Troubleshooting

Enter the IVs of the bird you hit into the search filters of owoow, and remove any Mark/Height filters that are currently in place. The goal is to find the bird you hit by changing the calibration values.

  • If your target is in Rain or Thunderstorm, change the Rain Tick value to whichever value you did not use (e.g, if you used 14 in Thunderstorm, try 16).
  • If your target is Moltres, confirm that Wailord is active/inactive, and that the Area Load calibration is properly configured (11 for inactive, 12 for active).
  • If your target is Moltres, confirm that you are using the correct table to calibrate your values. Game progression and game version is important.
  • If you still cannot find the bird you hit, consider tweaking the values inside the Menu Close/Fly NPCs fields until you find a match.
  • After finding your bird, retry and confirm that your new calibration values are correct. Report any new NPC values to me (Billo) on Discord (linked below).
