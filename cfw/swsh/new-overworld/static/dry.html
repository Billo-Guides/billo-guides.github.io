Settings Toggles:
  1. Search for a target frame. If you do not find any results consider increasing your search range or applying less strict search filters (e.g searching for a high IV Shiny will require the a high search range in most cases).

  2. Calibrate your NPC count and close the MenuCloseTimeline sub-window. This will enter your current NPC count into the main window, ensure the Consider Menu Close? checkbox remains checked.

  4. If you still cannot see a search result with both checkboxes active, this means that you cannot hit your target using menu close prediction. It is recommended to find a new target frame to avoid extra unnecessary complexity.

Advancing To The Target:

In the above example, a Shiny, Rare Mark Duraludon can be found in a cluster of advances from 26,000-26003, a cluster is a group of frames that produce the same end result. It is recommended to target clusters where possible as this will further increase the chances of being able to hit your target frame.

Step-by-step:
  1. Use any of the advancement methods found on this page to advance as close to your target frame as possible. It is recommended to use date skipping or wet weather advances when attempting to advance large distances.

  2. If you have moved location, leave yourself ample time to re-position yourself next to your target Pokemon, pause the game when you are a few hundred frames away from the target.

  3. Disconnect and reconnect to owow, this will start your advances from 0. Search for your target again after doing so, then use animation advances to reach the new target frame.

  4. Unpause the game. If you are using the Holding Direction? checkbox, ensure you are holding a direction on your control stick when unpausing, otherwise, ensure you are NOT holding a direction.

  5. Quickly run towards the spawner of your desired Pokemon, if done correctly, your Pokemon should have been spawned on the target frame, encounter it and check if it matches what is expected.
