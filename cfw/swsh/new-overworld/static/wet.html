Calibrating Necessary Values:
What Are "Rain Ticks"?

Rain ticks are the advances that occur as a result of Rain or Thunderstorm being active. Despite the sheer speed of advances caused by wet weather, the amount of advances that occur is predictable via calibration. Calibration can be done by using the Advanced Settings column of the main window.

Rain Tick Calibration
  1. Save the game when stood as close to the spawn border of your target Pokemon as possible, do not reposition yourself after this point.

  2. Calibrate your NPCs in any weather that is NOT Rain or Thunderstorm, then change the weather back after doing so. It is not possible to calibrate NPCs in wet weather.

  3. Ensure that the Consider Menu Close? and Holding Direction? checkboxes on the main window are active, and enter your calibrated NPC count into the NPCs field.

  4. In the Advanced Settings column, check the Raining/Thunderstorming? checkboxes. For now, keep the Rain Ticks value set to 0 for the sake of calibration.

  5. Open the Pokemon party list, then disconnect and reconnect to owoow to reset your advances to 0. This step is important for an accurate calibration.

  6. Back out from the party list to the main menu, then hold a direction on your control stick (hold whichever direction leads you to your target Pokemon).

  7. Unpause the game while holding the correct direction on your control stick, then spawn the Pokemon in the overworld and initiate the encounter.

  8. On owoow, press the Press the Read Encounter button, then press Copy IVs to Filters button after reading the encounter.

  9. In Advanced Settings, click the Calculate Rain Ticks button, if done correctly you should see a result pop-up. Enter the calibrated value into the Rain Ticks field.

  10. Once you have calibrated your NPC count and Rain Ticks, restart the game and reconnect to the program. You can now begin searching for target frames.

The calibration checkboxes (Consider Menu Close?, Holding Direction? & Rain Ticks) will account for the frames consumed when leaving the party list and closing the main pause menu. The search result table will show the exact frame you are expected to close the party list in order to spawn your target Pokemon in the overworld.


Troubleshooting - If multiple potential results appear during calibration, you will need to repeat the process a few times or adjust your position until a consistent Rain Ticks value is found. If you see "No results found" something was done incorrectly. Retry while following the above steps more carefully.

Advancing to the Target

In this example, a Shiny, Weather Mark Duraludon can be found in a cluster of advances from 9200-9203. Clusters are multiple frames that produce the same result, making them ideal for hitting the correct target frame. From here it is assumed that you have calibrated your current NPC and Rain Tick values.

Step-by-Step:
  1. Confirm that the Consider Menu Close? & Holding Direction? boxes have been checked, and that your NPC count and Rain Ticks have been entered properly.

  2. Advance towards your target using wet weather or date-skipping to maintain your current positioning and Rain Tick calibration.

  3. Take note of the amount of frames that have been advanced periodically. Stop a few hundred frames early and then pause the game.

  4. Open the Pokemon party list, this is where you will do the remainder of your advances using only animations.

  5. Disconnect and re-connect from owoow, this will reset your advances to 0. Keep the game paused on the party list from this point.

  6. Use animations to advance to your new target frame. Each L3 button input on the party list advances the RNG state by +1 (e.g., 100 inputs = 100 advances).

  7. Back out from the party list into the main pause menu, then hold a direction on your control stick, pointing towards the spawner of your target Pokemon.

  8. Unpause the game and quickly run toward the target Pokemon to spawn it in the overworld. Encounter the Pokemon and verify the results match what is expected.
