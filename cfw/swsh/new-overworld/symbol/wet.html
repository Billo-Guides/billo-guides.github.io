Calibrating Necessary Values:
What Are Rain Ticks?

Rain Ticks refer to the RNG advances that occur when Rain or Thunderstorm are the active weather condition. The advances are used to randomly place raindrops on screen. Despite the sheer speed of RNG advances caused by wet weather, the number of advances remains predictable through proper calibration. We will perform this calibration using the Advanced Settings column in the main window.

Rain Tick Calibration:
  1. Save the game while standing as close as possible to the spawn border of your target Pokémon. Do not reposition yourself after this point.

  2. Calibrate your NPC count in any weather that is NOT Rain or Thunderstorm, then change the weather back to Rain/Thunderstorm in both in-game and on owoow.

  3. Ensure the Consider Menu Close? and Holding Direction? checkboxes are enabled in the main window, and enter your calibrated NPC count in the NPCs field.

  4. In the Advanced Settings column, enable the Raining/Thunderstorming? checkboxes. Keep the Rain Ticks value set to 0 for now.

  5. Open the Pokémon party list, then disconnect and reconnect to owoow to reset your advances to 0. This step is important for accurate calibration.

  6. Back out of the party list to the main pause menu, then hold the control stick in the direction that leads towards the spawn point of your target Pokémon.

  7. Unpause the game while holding the correct direction, spawn the Pokémon in the overworld, and initiate the encounter with the Pokémon that spawned first.

  8. When in battle, click the Read Encounter button on owoow, then press Copy IVs to Filters after reading the encounter.

  9. If the encountered Pokémon is a different species to what is currently configured in the Encounter Settings column, update the Target and KO Count accordingly.

  10. In Advanced Settings, click Calculate Rain Ticks. If done correctly, a pop-up will display the result. Enter the calibrated value into the Rain Ticks field.

  11. After calibrating both the NPC count and Rain Ticks, restart the game and reconnect to owoow. You can now begin searching for target frames.

The calibration checkboxes (Consider Menu Close?, Holding Direction?, and Rain Ticks) account for frames consumed when leaving the Pokémon party list and closing the main pause menu. The search results table will display the exact frame at which you should close the party list and main pause menu to then spawn your target Pokémon in the overworld.


Troubleshooting - If multiple possible results appear during calibration, repeat the process a few times or adjust your position slightly until you find a consistent Rain Tick value. If you see "No results found", this is likely to be user error. Double-check each step and try again, ensuring you follow the instructions carefully.

Advancing to the Target

Holding a direction when closing the pause menu is mandatory for Rain/Thunderstorm targets, as this helps maintain a consistent Rain Tick value between attempts.

Recalibrate your NPC count if you have reset the game after a date advance. Rain Ticks should remain consistent as long as your position has not changed.

Step-by-Step:
  1. Ensure that the Consider Menu Close? and Holding Direction? checkboxes are enabled, and verify that your NPC count and Rain Tick values are correctly entered.

  2. Advance toward your target using wet weather or date skipping to maintain your position and Rain Tick calibration. DO NOT move the player.

  3. Monitor your progress and check the number of frames advanced periodically. Stop advancing once you are a few hundred frames before your target.

  4. Pause the game and open the Pokémon party list, this is where you will perform the remaining advances using only animations.

  5. Disconnect and reconnect to owoow, then search for your target again to reset your advances to 0. Keep the game paused on the party list from this point.

  6. Use animations to reach your exact target frame. Each L3 button input on the party list advances the RNG state by +1 (e.g., 100 inputs = 100 advances).

  7. Exit the party list back to the main pause menu, then hold a direction on your control stick pointing toward the spawner of your target Pokémon.

  8. Unpause the game and quickly run toward the target Pokémon to spawn it in the overworld. Encounter the Pokémon and verify that the results are a match.
