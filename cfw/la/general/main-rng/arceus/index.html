Arceus RNG Abuse

This section of the guide aims to provide a worked example for how to RNG abuse Arceus.

If you have made it this far, you should already have the initial setup completed and have a general understanding of how to use Moarencounterbots and the RNG calculation scripts, as well as understanding how to use PKHeX/Livehex to view Pokemon.

Don't understand how to use the tools/scripts yet? Click here to go back!
Getting Started:

Before anything else, you will need to calculate the rough delay for this encounter. The term "delay" is used to describe how long it take for the Pokemon to generate after the final A Press. We recommend disabling Autosave and to have a full team of 6 Pokemon so that your target Pokemon is sent to boxes. Sending the Pokemon to boxes will make it easier to view using Livehex.

Calculating Delay (Step-By-Step)
  • Initiate the Arceus boss battle and defeat it, then proceed to the screen of the final A button press, this will be the screen that shows the Arceus quest has been completed (This screen).
  • Press the Home button (⌂), then open Moarencounterbots and select EncBotRNGMonitorLA.
  • Head back to the Bots tab on Moarencounterbots and start the bot, if done correctly you should now see a seed has been produced in the Logs tab.
  • Paste your current seed into the Main RNG script, then search without filters. This will be the starting frame.
  • Unpause the game and continue mashing the A button to generate Arceus, then inspect it using Livehex.
  • Copy the PID of the Arceus and use the find function (Ctrl F) to search for the PID in your search results.
  • Take note of the frame that Arceus was generated on, this will be your delay for this attempt.
  • The delay for this encounter is not perfectly stable, it is recommended to repeat this process a few times to get a rough average delay that works for you.
Note - Defeating Arceus may be difficult at first, so we can use the delay calculation process to also become familiar with how to win the battle!

Figure 1 - Calculating Delay



After following the delay calculation steps, after searching for the PID of the Arceus we hit and matching the IVs/Nature, we can see that it was generated on frame 34. This means the delay for this attempt is 37. After 5 attempts we have the following delay values:

Attempt Delay Value
Attempt 1 34
Attempt 2 36
Attempt 3 43
Attempt 4 40
Attempt 5 37

Based on the table above, a safe delay to target would be between frame 34-43, these delay values are based on how quickly I was able to spam the A button to generate arceus, these values may vary for you!

Now that we have a rough delay, it's time to get to work on finding a suitable seed and target frame!

Figure 2 - Setting Filters



In this example the filters are set to search for any Arceus with 6 perfect IVs (31/31/31/31/31/31), you can be more specific with the filters if you wish, such as filtering for a specific nature, or optimal 0 IV values such as 0 Atk.

Figure 3 - Target Frame Found



Great! We found a 6IV Quirky Arceus in 18,241 advances in a cluster of 4, a cluster is a group of frames that produce the same end result, the larger the cluster the more chances you have of hitting the correct target frame.

We now need to advance to close to our target frame, you can now begin to monitor your RNG state by running EncBotRNGMonitorLA in Moarencounterbots, this will track the amount of advancements that have occurred in real time.

Figure 4 - RNG State Monitoring Using EncBotRNGMonitorLA



The RNG state at the Spear Pillar advances at a slow rate. By moving to a more populated area you can advance the RNG state at a much quicker rate, in the example image above the player is stood next to a large body of water in an area that is densely populated with Pokemon.

Consider leaving Spear Pillar and heading somewhere where the RNG state advances more rapidly if you need to cover a large amount of frame advancements. But be sure to save yourself enough time to reposition yourself at Spear Pillar and have enough frames left to defeat Arceus without overshooting your target.

Example

If it takes you roughly 3,000 frames to get back to Spear Pillar from your position, and roughly 7,000 frames to defeat Arceus, start to head back to Spear Pillar at roughly 10,000 frames away from your target, leave enough time to reposition yourself to avoid overshooting!

Figure 5 - Advancing To The Target



We are now back at Spear Pillar and 6,255 frames away from the target, it can take up to 10,000 advances to defeat the Arceus boss on average, it is recommended to check how long it takes you to win, as any time during or after the Arceus battle will advance the RNG state slowly.

On average it takes me 5,000-7,000 frames to defeat the boss, so there is enough time to defeat it and position myself on the screen of the final A press and ultimately to avoid overshooting the target frame.

Figure 6 - Screen Of Final Button Press



Get to the screen of the final A press (pictured above), and let EncBotRNGMonitorLA run until the Logs tab displays the correct target frame. You can use the Home button (⌂) to close in on your target frame at your own pace.

Figure 7 - Hitting The Target Frame



Here we can see the target is now on frame 217 after re-entering the seed, and that the calculated delay is 36. Once the Sysbot log output displays that we are on frame 182, this means that the target frame has been reached.

x (earliest target frame) - y (delay) = z (frame to press A)

We will now begin spamming the A button as quickly as possible to re-open the game and spam through the menus, this will provide the best chance of hitting the correct target frame.

Figure 8 - Result Found!



We hit our target frame and have secured a 6IV Arceus! That's all there is to it!
