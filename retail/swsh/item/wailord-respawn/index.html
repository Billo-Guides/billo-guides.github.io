Wailord Respawn RNG (RETAIL)

A small guide for respawning the Isle of Armor Wailord.

The enormous Isle of Armor Wailord is visible in the Workout Sea when you first arrive on the Isle of Armor. Once defeated or captured, it does not return normally with date skipping like normal "strong spawns." Instead, the chance of it respawning is 1/100 each fresh day that you first visit the IoA map. Using RNG manipulation, we can respawn the signature IoA Wailord much more easily.

Important - From here, we assume that you are familiar with key concepts described on the main page. If you are unsure about seed monitoring, advancement methods, or NPC calibration, please review the relevant sections before continuing.

Figure 1 - Game Setup

Getting Started (Game Setup)
  1. You will need to date skip so that Isle of Armor considers it a new day. The method you will use depends on if you have the ability to perform Link Battles, either locally or via NSO.
    • If you can activate the PvP date skipping exploit, head to the Isle of Armor, enter the train station, and perform a date skip this way.
    • If you cannot use a Link Battle, fly to the train station on IoA, perform a date skip using a nearby Max Raid den, and then walk or bike into the train station without reloading the map.
  2. Now that you are inside the Isle of Armor trains tation, identify your seed and calibrate your menu NPC count; a common NPC value for the train station is 6.
  3. Save the game.

Wailord has one chance to respawn per day. The check occurs the first time the player enters the Isle of Armor overworld. For this reason, we are required to perform a date skip while standing inside the train station if we have already entered the Isle of Armor overworld for the day. After performing a date skip, we can then save to preserve the new day state.


Figure 2 - RNG Tool Setup

Getting Started (Tool Setup)
  1. At the top of the main owoow window, click Wailord Respawn to open the Wailord Respawn generator in a subwindow.
  2. Enter the number of advances you want to search in the + field. You will usually find any desired result in 5,000 advances or fewer.
  3. Tick the Consider Menu Close? and Holding Direction? checkboxes, then enter the NPC count that was calibrated earlier (mostly likely 6). You can now begin searching for targets.

If you do not find any results, you will need to increase your search range, change your search filters to be less restrictive, or reset the game for a better seed.


Figure 3 - Target Found!

Advancing Towards a Target

In this example, a frame that will respawn Wailord can be seen in a {% include tooltips/clusters.html %} from advances 50-53. These advances are highlighted yellow, which means that Wailord will respawn if we enter the Isle of Armor main map on any of them for the first time that day.

Step-by-Step
  1. Advance toward your target using NPC activation, menu close, or attack animations depending on how close it is.
  2. Once you are roughly 100-200 advances before your target, stand close to the train station exit so that holding down on your joystick will cause your character to leave the station and trigger the loading screen.
  3. Open the X Menu to pause the game.
  4. Re-identify your current seed. Click Update Seeds on the main window of owoow after re-identification, which will send your current seed to the active subwindow.
  5. Ensure that the Consider Menu Close? and Holding Direction? checkboxes are checked in the Wailord Respawn subwindow, and confirm that your NPC count has been entered correctly. Search for your target again.
  6. From this point, use attack animations to reach the exact target frame.
  7. Close the Pokémon window so you are on the main X Menu.
  8. Hold the left joystick downwards and then unpause the game by pressing B. Release the joystick the moment you see the black loading screen.
  9. If you did this correctly, the player character should immediately leave the train station and enter the Isle of Armor overworld. You will see that Wailord has been properly respawned.
