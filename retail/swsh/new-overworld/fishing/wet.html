Calibrating Necessary Values:
What Are "Rain Ticks"?

Rain ticks are the advances that occur as a result of Rain or Thunderstorm being active. Despite the sheer speed of advances caused by wet weather, the amount of advances that occur is predictable via calibration. Calibration can be done by using the Advanced Settings column of the main window.

Rain Tick Calibration
  1. Save the game when stood as close to the spawn border of your target Pokemon as possible, do not reposition yourself after this point.

  2. Pause the game and identify your seed using the Retail Seed Finder, then calibrate your NPC count in any weather that is NOT Rain or Thunderstorm.

  3. Set your weather back to Rain/Thunderstorm on both owoow and in-game. Double check that the weather conditions are correct.

  4. Ensure that the Consider Menu Close? and Holding Direction? checkboxes on the main window are active, and enter your calibrated NPC count into the NPCs field.

  5. In the Advanced Settings column, check the Raining/Thunderstorming? checkboxes. For now, keep the Rain Ticks value set to 0 for the sake of calibration.

  6. Open the Pokemon party list, then re-identify your seed to reset your advances to 0. This step is important for an accurate calibration.

  7. Back out from the party list to the main menu, then hold a direction on your control stick (hold whichever direction leads you towards the fishing pool).

  8. Unpause the game while holding the correct direction on your control stick, then spawn the fishing pool in the overworld and initiate the encounter.

  9. Capture the Pokemon and inspect the IVs using a calculator (e.g. Serebii or Showdown Teambuilder), then enter them into the search filters of owoow.

  10. Change the species inside the Encounter Settings column to match the species you are in battle with.

  11. In Advanced Settings, click the Calculate Rain Ticks button, if done correctly you should see a result pop-up. Enter the calibrated value into the Rain Ticks field.

  12. Once you have calibrated your NPC count and Rain Ticks, restart the game and find your seed again. You can now begin searching for target frames.

The calibration checkboxes (Consider Menu Close?, Holding Direction? & Rain Ticks) will account for the frames consumed when leaving the party list and closing the main pause menu. The search result table will show the exact frame you are expected to close the party list in order to spawn your target Pokemon in the overworld.


Troubleshooting - If multiple potential results appear during calibration, you will need to repeat the process a few times or adjust your position until a consistent Rain Ticks value is found. If you see "No results found" something was done incorrectly. Retry while following the above steps more carefully.

Step-by-Step:
  1. Confirm that the Consider Menu Close? & Holding Direction? boxes have been checked, and that your NPC count and Rain Ticks have been entered properly.

  2. Advance towards your target using wet weather or the PvP date-skipping exploit to maintain your current positioning and Rain Tick calibration.

  3. Re-identify your seed periodically to prevent overshooting. Stop advancing a few hundred frames before your target, then pause the game and open the party list.

  4. Re-identify your seed on this screen and search for your target frame again, this will reset your advances to 0. Keep the game paused on the party list from this point.

  5. Use animations to advance to your new target frame. Each L3 button input on the party list advances the RNG state by +1 (e.g., 100 inputs = 100 advances).

  6. Back out from the party list into the main pause menu, then hold a direction on your control stick (pointing towards the spawner of your target Pokemon).

  7. Quickly run toward the fishing pool to spawn it in the overworld. Throw in your rod and reel the Pokemon in, then verify the results match what is expected.
