Overworld Static Encounters

Worked examples for manipulating Static encounters in any weather condition.

Static spawners consistently produce the same species until the weather in the area changes, which can occur at midnight (00:00). Be mindful of the current system time to avoid spawning unexpected Pokemon due to incorrect weather conditions.

Important - It is assumed that you are familiar with the basic concepts outlined on the main page of this guide. If you are unsure about topics such as seed finding, advancement methods, or NPC calibration, please revisit the relevant pages before starting.
Figure 1 - RNG Tool Setup

Getting Started (Tool Setup):
  1. In the main window, enter your TID/SID, select your game version, and check the Shiny/Mark Charm checkboxes if these items are present in your save file.

  2. Select Encounter Lookup from the toolbar at the top of the owoow main window to open the sub-window, then type the name of your desired species.

  3. Search for a STATIC encounter, then enter the Location/Weather/Species information into the Encounter Settings section of the main window.

  4. Adjust your search range (the + field in Encounter Settings), then configure your search filters such as filtering for Shiny, Mark, IVs, or Height.

At this point it is assumed that you have entered your save file details into the main window, and have your desired target/location/weather condition in mind. You do not need to search for a target frame yet.


Figure 2 - Game Setup

Game Setup:
  1. Remove any Pokemon that are currently stored in the {% include tooltips/daycare.html %} then fly to the required location and run around until you have found the spawner that contains your desired Pokemon.

  2. Move far enough away to completely despawn your target Pokemon in-game, set the required weather condition, then save the game. Aim to be as close to the Pokemon as possible without actually spawning it in the overworld.

  3. If you cannot find a good position, you can knock out any additional static spawners that are blocking your path. Those will remain despawned as long as you do not move far away or reload the area.

  4. Once you are positioned correctly, add your desired lead Pokemon to your party and then save the game once more. Detailed information about lead abilities and their use cases can be found by clicking the button below.

To confirm that you have ideal positioning, look for a dust cloud or puddle at the foot of the Pokemon when it appears. If these effects are not visible, you are too close to the spawner (it may be hard to see this effect if your target Pokemon is in grass or flying).
