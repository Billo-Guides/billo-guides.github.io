Overworld Static Encounters

Worked examples for manipulating Static encounters in any weather condition.

Static spawners consistently generate the same species of Pokémon until the weather changes, which can occur at midnight (00:00). Be mindful of your system time to prevent unexpected Pokémon spawns due to incorrect weather conditions.

Important - This guide assumes you are already familiar with the basic concepts outlined on the main page. If you're unsure about topics such as seed finding, advancement methods, or NPC calibration, please revisit those pages before continuing.
Figure 1 - RNG Tool Setup

Getting Started (Tool Setup):
  1. In the main window, enter your TID/SID, select your game version, and check the Shiny/Mark Charm checkboxes if these items are present in your save file.

  2. Click Encounter Lookup in the top toolbar to open a sub-window. Enter the desired species name in the search field and select a STATIC encounter.

  3. Close the Encounter Lookup window, then input the relevant Location, Weather, and Species information into the Encounter Settings section.

  4. Adjust your search range using the + field in Encounter Settings, then configure additional search filters such as Shiny, Marks, IVs, or Height.

Figure 2 - Game Setup

Game Setup:
  1. Remove any Pokémon currently stored in the {% include tooltips/daycare.html %} then fly to the required location and run around until you have found the spawner that contains your desired Pokémon.

  2. Move far enough away to completely despawn your target Pokémon, set the required weather condition, then save the game. Position yourself as close to the Pokémon's spawn border as possible without actually spawning it in the overworld.

  3. If you cannot find an optimal position, you can knock out any additional static spawners blocking your path. These will remain despawned as long as you do not move too far away or reload the area.

  4. Once positioned correctly, add your desired lead Pokémon to your party and save the game again. For detailed information on lead abilities and their effects, click the button below.

To confirm ideal positioning, look for a dust cloud or puddle beneath the Pokémon when it spawns. If these effects are not visible, you are standing too close to the spawner. (Note: This may be difficult to see if your target Pokémon is in tall grass or flying.)
