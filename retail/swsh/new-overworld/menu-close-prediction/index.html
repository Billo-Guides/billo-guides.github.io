Menu Close Prediction

Understanding the concept of menu close prediction and how to calibrate your NPC count on RETAIL.

This page contains information about calibrating your NPC count, and using that knowledge to grant yourself the best possible chance of hitting a target frame/Pokemon. Menu close prediction is used extensively throughout this guide, so it is very important to have an understanding of how to use the technique to your advantage.

Important - By this stage you should also understand how to find and monitor your current seed. If you have not yet read the provided information on this topic, go back and review the material.

Figure 1 - Finding Your Current Seed

Finding Your Current Seed:

In the above image, we have identified our current seed using the Retail Seed Finder. The game is also paused on the party summary screen after doing so (if you have already identified your current seed, simply re-identify your seed instead).


Figure 2 - Menu Close Timeline Overview

Menu Close Timeline Overview:

Checking the Consider Menu Close? box unlocks the Calibrate NPCs button, clicking the button will open a sub-window. This is where you determine the number of active NPCs in your location.

When the window opens, the seed fields will be automatically populated with your current seed. If you have not yet configured the Encounter Settings on the main window, you will need to manually set the weather to match your current area.


Figure 3 - Menu Close Demonstration

Menu Close Timeline Overview:

Closing and re-opening the pause menu as demonstrated in the above image will advance the RNG state, the amount of advances consumed from this action will vary depending on your current NPC count.

After doing this, re-identify your seed using the Retail Tools section of the main window. DO NOT CLICK THE UPDATE STATES BUTTON YET.


Figure 4 - Seed Re-identification

Seed Re-identification:

After re-identifying your seed in the Retail Tools section the Adv.: field displays two values. The first box contains the total number of advances, and the second box contains the number of animations used to re-identify your seed.

To find the number of advances consumed by closing the pause menu, simply subtract the number of advances used to re-identify your seed from the total number of advancements that have occured.

Example - 6 animations were used to re-identify your seed, and 11 advances have been consumed in total, we can now confirm that 5 advances were consumed from closing the pause menu.


Figure 5 - Finding NPC Count

Finding NPC Count:

Head back to the MenuCloseTimeline window, if this is your first time opening this window, set the Initial Adv. field to 0 and set your current weather condition. Now click the Search button to produce a table.

The goal is to make the value in the Jump column match the amount of advances that were consumed from closing the pause menu by tweaking the NPC count. In this case, we will tweak the NPC count until the first result in the table displays a +5 Jump value, this will give us an idea of the current NPC count.


Figure 6 - NPC Count Found

Click Update States at this point, and repeat this process 2-3 times to ensure that the NPC count matches correctly. In this case, our NPC count is 3. Keep in mind that NPC count is subject to change based on positioning and location, if you switch either of this two variables you will need to re-calibrate.

Great! You should now have a basic understanding of how to calibrate NPC counts, and how to apply that knowledge to accurately hit target frames (you can leave this page open for future reference).
