Menu Close Prediction

Understanding the concept of menu close prediction and how to calibrate your NPC count on RETAIL.

This page contains information about calibrating your NPC count, and using that knowledge to grant yourself the best possible chance of hitting a target frame/Pokemon. Menu close prediction is used extensively throughout this guide, so it is very important to have an understanding of how to use the technique to your advantage.

Important - By this stage you should also understand how to find and monitor your current seed. If you have not yet read the provided information on this topic, go back and review the material.
Go back to finding your seed
Figure 1 - Finding Your Current Seed

In Figure 1, we have identified our current seed using the Retail Seed Finder. The game is also paused on the party summary screen after doing so (if you have already identified your current seed, simply re-identify your seed instead).

Figure 2 - Menu Close Timeline

Clicking the Consider Menu Close? checkbox on the main window gains access to the Calibrate NPCs button, clicking this button opens a sub-window. Here is where we will determine the amount of NPCs currently active in our location.

Upon opening the window you should see that your seed has been entered into the relevant fields automatically. If you have not yet entered any information into the Encounter Settings on the main window, you will need to change the weather condition to match the weather in your current area.

Figure 3 - Menu Close Demonstration

Close and re-open the pause menu quickly, this will advance the current RNG state (the amount will vary depending on the NPC count). After doing this, re-identify your seed using the Retail Tools section of the main window. DO NOT UPDATE STATES YET.

Figure 4 - Seed re-identification

The Adv.: field displays two values after re-identifying your seed. The first box contains the total number of advances, and the second box contains the number of animations used to re-identify. Subtract the animations from the total number of advances, this will give you the # of advances consumed from closing the pause menu.

Example

6 animations were used to re-identify the seed, and a total of 11 advances were consumed throughout the whole process, we can now confirm that 5 advances were consumed from closing the pause menu.

Figure 5 - Finding NPC Count

After clicking the Search button, a table will appear. We now need to tweak the value inside the NPCs box, and observe the Jump column of the table. The goal is to make the value in the Jump column match the amount of advances that were consumed from closing the pause menu.

Earlier, we calculated that 5 advances were consumed from closing the pause menu, so we will tweak the value in the NPCs field until we see a +5 Jump inside the first row of the table, this will give us an idea of the current NPC count.

Figure 6 - NPC Count Found

Click Update States at this point, and repeat this process 2-3 times to ensure that the NPC count matches correctly. In this case, our NPC count is x. Keep in mind that NPC count is subject to change based on positioning and location, if you switch either of this two variables you will need to re-calibrate.

Closing the MenuCloseTimeline will enter your calibrated NPC value into the main window automatically, keep this in mind.

Great! You should now have a basic understanding of how to calibrate NPC counts, and how to apply that knowledge to accurately hit target frames (you can leave this page open for future reference).
