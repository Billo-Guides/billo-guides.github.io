Sword/Shield Roaming RNG

This guide explains how to RNG abuse Pokémon from Roaming encounters (Galarian Articuno, Zapdos & Moltres). It covers the entire process, from choosing a location to obtaining the desired Pokémon.

Each bird spawns in one of the Wild Area, Articuno can be found in Crown Tundra, Zapdos can be found in base Wild Area, and Moltres can be found in the Isle of Armor, you must complete the Crown Tundra main quest to spawn these encounters in the overworld.

From here it is assumed that you have read the initial page of this guide and have a general idea of how to use SWSH OWRNG Generator GUI.

Picked the wrong type of encounter? Click here to go back!
Getting Started:
  1. Open SWSH_OWRNG_Generator_GUI, then click on the Encounter Lookup button at the top of the window to open the Encounter Lookup tab.

  2. Type the species name into the empty search field to bring up a list of encounters for that specific Pokémon. Select a row that appeals to you by left clicking it.

  3. Confirm that the filters have been set properly inside the Main Window, encounter filters can be found on the left-hand side.

  4. Set your search filters inside the middle portion of the Main Window. You can filter for specific Marks, IVs, Nature, etc.

  5. Fly to the correct location in game and save.
Avoid rain or thunderstorm when picking an encounter where possible, rain causes advancements to occur at an extreme rate, making it difficult to hit target frames.

The Process

The end goal is to fly into the area that contains our chosen bird at the perfect time, to spawn it on the calculated target frame provided by the generator search results. Once a roaming bird spawns in the overworld, its stats and details are fixed until you reload the area.

Ensure your encounter and search filters are properly configured before continuing (see Figure 1). The advancement methods used to achieve the result are listed in the table found on the main page.

Undesirable result?
  1. Disconnect from the program, and restart the game to refresh the seed.
  2. Enter the overworld, pause the game, then reconnect to the program.
  3. Search for a new target frame, repeat this process until a desirable result shows in the generator search results.

Advancing Towards the Target!

First, you will need to calculate the rough delay for the encounter. Doing this step will provide the best possible chances of hitting your target frame. Delay is the difference in advancements between spawning the Pokémon in the overworld and the actual frame the Pokémon was generated on.

Delay Calculation
Delay Calculation
  1. When standing at the recommended fly point of your desired bird, pause the game and begin identifying your current seed.

  2. Open the map without closing the pause menu, then fly into the area that contains your chosen bird.

  3. Chase it down and initiate the encounter, capture the Pokémon so that you can view the IVs/Nature more accurately.

  4. After capturing the Pokémon, enter the stats into any IV calculator to get a rough idea of the IVs of the Pokémon you hit. Also take note of the nature of the Pokémon you found.

  5. Find the Pokémon you hit in the search results of the GUI, this should be relatively close to your start frame. Check to see which advancement generated your Pokémon.

  6. Subtract the amount of advancements that were consumed by identifying your current seed, if this is your initial identification this will be 128.

  7. This will be your delay for this encounter, for example, if it took 128 advancements to identify your seed, and your bird was generated on advancement 160, your delay would be 32 (128+32=160).

  8. Since delay is variable for roaming encounters, it is recommended to repeat this process a few times to get a rough average delay that is consistent for you.
Recommended starting delays:
  • Articuno - 130-140
  • Zapdos - 20-30
  • Moltres - 60-80

Take note of the calculated delay, you will need this information in order to reach the correct target frame later. Do not change your fly point after calculating the delay, as this is subject to change depending on positioning.

Hitting your target frame
Step-by-step tutorial:

  1. Advance towards your target frame using any of the methods listed inside the table on main page of this guide. Consider day skipping to advance large distances.

  2. Once you are within a few hundred frames of your target, stand in a quiet location.

  3. Pause the game using the X button, re-identify your current seed using the Retail Advances Tracker, then search for your target again.

    4. From this point it's recommended to only advance using animations as they only advance in small increments, making them easy to keep track of.

    It's also recommended to have one Pokemon on your party to decrease the amount of advancements that will occur from opening the map from the pause menu.

  4. Open and close the map, and re-identify your current seed. Take note of how many advances occur from doing so, repeat this step multiple times and take an average number from the results.

  5. Open the party menu and advance to your target frame using animations, here's where we need to factor in the amount of advances that pass from closing the map.

    6. Example: If your target frame is on advance 100, the generation delay is 30, and the average amount of advancements from opening the town map is 2, we will need to open the town map on advance 68 in order to hit the target frame. (68+2+30=100)

  6. Open the map and fly into the area to spawn your bird! Encounter and capture it to confirm you hit your target frame.

    7. You can be creative with the methods you use to advance the RNG state. The above method is the recommended method that provides the most consistency of hitting target frames for roaming encounters.
