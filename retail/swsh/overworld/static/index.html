Sword/Shield Static RNG

This guide explains how to RNG abuse Pokémon from Static encounters. It covers the entire process, from choosing a location to obtaining the desired Pokémon.

Static spawners consistently produce the same species until the weather changes, which can occur at midnight (00:00). Be mindful of the Switch system time to avoid spawning unexpected Pokémon due to incorrect weather conditions.

From here it is assumed that you have read the initial page of this guide and have a general idea of how to use SWSH OWRNG Generator GUI, as well as understanding how to find and monitor your seed.

Getting Started:
  1. Open SWSH_OWRNG_Generator_GUI, then type your 5 digit TID/SID into the correct fields of the main window if you plan to RNG abuse shiny Pokémon.

  2. Open the Encounter Lookup sub window, then type the species name of the Pokémon you plan to RNG abuse.

  3. Select a row that appeals to you from the list of encounters, this will fill in all of the encounter filters on the main window automatically.

  4. Confirm that the filters have been set properly inside the Main Window, encounter filters can be found on the left-hand side.

  5. Set your search filters inside the middle portion of the Main Window. You can filter for specific Marks, IVs, Shininess, etc.

  6. Fly to the correct location in-game and save. Make sure you are standing far enough away from your target Pokémon to properly despawn it.

Avoid rain or thunderstorm when picking an encounter where possible, rain causes advancements to occur at an extreme rate, making it difficult to hit target frames.

The Process

The end goal is to spawn the Pokémon on the calculated target frame provided by the generator search results. Once a Pokémon spawns in the overworld, its stats and details are fixed.

Ensure your encounter and search filters are properly configured before continuing (see Figure 1). The advancement methods used to achieve the result are listed in the table found on the main page.

Game Setup:

For Static encounters, stand where there is a clear, straight line between you and the target Pokémon. Avoid areas that trigger other Wild/Static spawns. Once you have good positioning, calculate the amount of NPCs using the method described on the main page of the guide.

The location in the above image is ideal: Minimal NPCs, no extra spawned Pokémon, and a clear line to the target spawner.

Giant's Bed, in the Crown Tundra Wild Area, is notably "quiet" in calm weather. Remember, advancement speed (aka noise) depends on location, NPC count, and weather. The more quiet the area, the better.

If you can't find a good spot, knock out any static spawns blocking your path. They will remain despawned until you return after a date change.

Figure 3 - Seed identification


You cannot search for targets without knowing your current seed. This is achieved by using the Retail Seed Finder window. If you already know how to find and monitor your seed you can skip this section and begin finding your current seed.

Seed (Re)Identification:
How to identify current seeds using the Retail Seed Finder:

  1. Open the Retail Seed Finder window and open the pause menu in-game.

  2. Open the Pokemon party menu, select a Pokemon, then click summary.

  3. Begin making your Pokemon animate by using the L3 button, take note of the type of animation. (Physical or special).

  4. If the animation was physical, click the (0)Physical button, if the animation was special, click the (1)Special button.

    If you don't feel like clicking the button, you can use your keyboard too! Simply type 0 or , for a physical animation, and 1 or . for a special animation.

  5. Repeat this process 128 times, if done correctly your current seeds will now be visible on-screen. Click Update Main Form. At this stage it is safe to close the Retail Seed Finder window.

  6. After identifying your current seed you can now begin searching for target frames!
How to re-identify current seeds using the Retail Advances Tracker:

  1. Click the Generate Pattern button on the main window after identifying your current seed.

  2. Open the Pokemon party menu, select a Pokemon, then click summary.

  3. Record 10-20 animations until you are left with 1 result.

  4. Click Update States after doing so to input your new current seed into the State[0] and Stete[1] fields automatically.

  5. Search for your target frame again and confirm the seed re-identification was correct.
Seed Identification Troubleshooting:
If you do not find a result after re-identification, or your target frame disappears, this is because either:

  • The initial seed identification (using the Retail Seed Finder) was incorrect. Which is a result of not recording the animations properly.
  • The re-identification animations were not recorded properly, and produced an invalid result.
To resolve this you will need to re-identify your current seed from the start, pay close attention to the sequence of animations to avoid producing incorrect results!

Seed re-identification plays a big role in advancing towards a target frame, and also helps to find out how many advancements will occur from a specific in-game action.

If your results are not desirable, you will need to reset the game and identify your current seeds again.

Hitting your target frame
Step-by-step tutorial:

  1. Advance towards your target frame using any of the methods listed inside the table on main page of this guide. Consider day skipping to advance large distances.

  2. Once you are within a few thousand frames of your target, stand near to the spawn point of the Pokémon you are hunting.

  3. Pause the game using the X button, re-identify your current seed using the Retail Advances Tracker, then search for your target again.

    4. From this point it's recommended to only advance using menu close & animations as they only advance in small increments, making them easy to keep track of.

  4. Open the Menu Close Advances Viewer window and work out the amount of NPCs that are in the area, (NPC calculation is explained on the main page if you are unsure.) Take note of this number as you will need it later.

  5. Re-identify your current seed once again. You should be roughly a few hundred frames away from the target at this stage.

  6. Open the Menu Close Advances Viewer window again and enter your target frame & amount of NPCs into the correct fields then search again.

    7. If you are unable to find your target frame in the search results, this means the target cannot be hit based on your seed. This can be resolved using animations to advance the RNG state slightly, then searching again.

    How To:
    1. Open the Pokemon summary screen and press the L3 button to advance the RNG state by a single frame.
    2. Open the main window, and set the IV/Shiny/Mark filters to the default settings.
    3. Set the search radius to a small amount, then press search.
    4. You should now be able to see (and track) your current seed (State0 and State1 based on your search results.
    5. Enter your new current seed, target frame, and the amount of NPCs calculated earlier into the Retail Menu Advances Viewer window, and search again.

  7. Use animations to advance to the a point where closing the pause menu once will land directly onto your target frame, according to the Menu Close Advances Viewer prediction.

  8. Close the pause menu and quickly run towards the spawn point of your Pokémon! Encounter and capture it, make sure all the internal details match the output shown on the generator.

    9. You can be creative with the methods you use to advance the RNG state. Menu close prediction is not necessary but will make for a much easier time hitting target frames and is extremely helpful in areas of the game where the RNG advances at a quick pace.
