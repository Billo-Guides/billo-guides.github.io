Hidden Encounter RNG (Retail)

If you made it this far, it must mean you have an interest in RNG abusing Pokémon from hidden encounters! This page will cover the whole process, from finding a desirable location to achieving a final result.

Getting Started:

Before starting, do the following:
  • Open the GUI and click on the Encounter Lookup button at the top of the main window. This will open the Encounter Lookup tab.
  • Type the species name of the Pokémon you are hunting into the empty field, this will bring up a list of every possible encounter for that Pokémon.
  • Scroll down the list of encounters and click on one of the rows displaying a HIDDEN encounter, this will automatically enter the information into the main window.
  • Enter your 5 digit TID/SID into the fields on the main window, select Shiny Charm & Mark charm if you have them present in your save file.
  • Head to the location in-game, change the date until you meet the correct weather condition, then stand near to a patch of grass in the area.
  • Run far enough away to despawn the Pokémon and save, then press the X button to pause the game.
It's suggested to pick an encounter in a quiet area with calm weather to reduce instability. Noise is area specific, you should test how noisy an area is before attempting to RNG abuse Pokémon in it.

Identifying your current seeds:

It's impossible to search for results without knowing the current seeds, you can identify your current seed through the Retail Seed Finder window of the generator, open it and begin recording your sequence of animations.

Identifying your current seed using Retail Seed Finder:
  1. Open the game and enter the overworld.
  2. Pause the game using X, click Pokémon, then view the summary of any of your Pokémon.
  3. Open the Retail Seed Finder window and have your Pokémon animate.
  4. Click (or type) 0 if your animation was physical.
  5. Click (or type) 1 if your animation was special.
  6. Repeat this process 128 times and your seed will be generated. Click Update Main Form and exit back to the main window.
  7. Search for a target frame.
This can seem like a lengthy and tedious process at first glance (it kinda is), for this reason it's advised to remap your controller button layout if you are able to do so, such as remapping the L3 button to Y. This is done through the Switch system settings.

Once you know your seed you can easily re-identify your current state within 10-20 animations, this is done using the Retail Advances Tracker feature found on the main window.

Re-identifying your current RNG states using the Retail Advances Tracker:
  1. Click Generate Pattern once you have found your seeds using Retail Seed Finder.
  2. Pause the game using X, click Pokémon, then view the summary of any of your Pokémon and have it do some animations.
  3. Click (or type) 0 if your animation was physical.
  4. Click (or type) 1 if your animation was special.
  5. Repeat roughly 10-20 times until you are given 1 possible result. Click Update States to automatically paste your new seeds into the correct fields.
  6. Search for your target again. If you see your target on the generator again that means the original seed identification was correct and you can continue following the guide.
  7. If you cannot find your original target this means seed identification was incorrect and needs to be redone.
Seed re-identification plays a huge role, it helps us keep track of the amount of frame advancements between searches. This is extremely helpful for closing in on a specific target frame or finding out how many advancements a specific action achieves.

The generator does not currently provide support for mechanics that boost the encounter rates of specific Pokémon. This includes Dexrec boosts or abilities like Storm Drain. Using spawn boosters like these may output incorrect results.

With all the required reading out of the way, it's time to get started!

Time to get to work!

The goal is to walk towards into grass and spawn a hidden encounter on the calculated target frame given by the generator. The advancement methods used are listed in detail on the main page of this guide.

We will be aiming for a Shiny Sinistea-Antique in Glimwood Tangle. You can be more specific with the filters if you wish, such as filtering for a specific mark or specific IVs.

Using Sinistea as the example:
- The Encounter Lookup tab tells us that Sinistea-Antique has an encounter slot range of 99-99, a level range of 34-36 and is a hidden encounter in all weather. It also tells us Sinistea-Antique does not hold a random item and has 0 potential Egg Moves.
- Clicking the row that applies to our encounter will enter all the information we need into the main window.
- KO count is irrelevant as hidden encounters cannot be Brilliant Aura.

Hidden encounters have a chance of spawning per step taken while in grass. If the encounter does not spawn in the first 1-2 steps there is a good chance that you will miss your target frame.

Once a Pokémon is spawned in the grass the stats and internal details (such as PID/EC) are generated and set in stone, this is why it is only important to spawn the hidden encounter on the correct frame. Don't let the shaking grass despawn after you spawn it in the overworld!

It's common to see "clusters" or "groups" of frames that produce the same result when searching for a target. Aiming for large clusters is recommended as this will provide more chances for the player to spawn the Pokémon on the correct frame.

Advancing towards the target:
The generator displays the target on frame 3451-3453, based on local testing the delay for this hidden encounter is 3. This means we will aim to walk into the grass on frame 3448-3450.

Step by step:
  1. Advance towards the target frame using bike, NPCs or by spawning overworld Pokémon in the area.

    2. If your target is a large number of frames away consider using the day skipping method described on the main page of this guide.

  2. Once you believe you are close to the target (within ~500 frames), stand near to the spawn point of the Pokémon you are hunting and re-identify your current seed.

    3. Re-identify your current seed using the Pokémon summary screen, not by using the party menu.

    From this point it's recommended to only advance using menu close, animation and bike advancements as they only advance in small increments, making them easy to keep track of.

  3. Quickly close and reopen the pause menu, re-identify your current seed and pay attention to the number of advancements that have passed, repeat this step roughly 5 times.

    4. It's important to know the average number of frames that will advance from closing the pause menu, we will eventually need to account for this to hit the correct target frame.

  4. Advance closer to your target frame (within ~100 frames this time) and open the pause menu again, open the Pokémon summary page and re-identify your current seed once more.

    5. Make sure you are not on your bike at this point, you should also have a calculator available to use to keep track of inputs/advancements.

    From this point it's recommended to only advance using animations from the party menu, this provide a stable +1 advancement per input.

  5. Open the party menu and advance to your target frame, you can use a calculator to keep track of inputs. Here's where we need to factor in the amount of advances that pass from closing the pause menu and the delay of the area.

    6. For example, if the area has a base delay of 3, and 8 frames advance from closing the pause menu (average) and the target was 100 advances away, we would advance to frame 89 (100-11) using animations, then closing the pause menu would pick up the 3 advancements we need to hit the target frame.

  6. Close the pause menu and quickly run into the grass to try to spawn a hidden encounter! If successful, encounter/capture it and make sure it matches the output shown on the generator.

    7. Remember not to let it despawn! If an encounter did not spawn you will have to begin looking for a new target frame.
You can be creative with the methods you use to advance the RNG state.

Troubleshooting Encounters

This section covers how to troubleshoot encounters and delays. If you keep missing targets, spawning in the wrong Pokémon, or can't find your result on the generator at all, this is the place to be.

Missing Targets:

The commonly occuring reasons for missing a target Pokémon are:
  • Encounter did not spawn.
  • Encounter spawned too late due to mistiming.
  • There is a delay left unaccounted for.
  • Frame was "hit" but an incorrect species of Pokémon was encountered.
  • Information in the main window was not completely accurate.
Encounter did not spawn or spawned too late...
This is a common issue players will face when tackling hidden encounters and is likely due to mistiming when spawning the Pokémon. Retry and search for a new target frame and make sure your player positioning is correct.

Standing directly next to the grass is advised as you will be able to spawn a hidden encounter in 1-2 steps. You will know if you are standing too close when the player starts the crouching animation, this is something we want to avoid.

Troubleshooting delay...
For the purposes of this guide, the term "delay" is used to describe the frame difference between the Pokémon we encountered and the frame the encounter was initiated on.

Most hidden encounters need to account for some delay, this applies most to encounters in extreme weather conditions or in an otherwise unstable environment, like having other spawned Pokémon present.

Delay Calc - frame Pokemon was generated - frame encounter was initiated = delay

We can use an IV calculator to confirm the exact frame the encounter was generated on and calculate the delay from there. Here's how it's done!
  • Aim for a target, such as any shiny Pokémon.
  • Run into the grass and spawn a hidden encounter. Take note of the frame the Pokémon was spawned on.
  • Encounter the Pokémon and capture it, then calculate the exact IVs using an IV calculator such as the this one by Serebii.
  • If you didn't find the Pokémon that you expected, search for the specific IVs of the result you found and find the frame you hit.
  • Subtract your the frame the Pokémon was spawned on from the frame you actually hit.
  • This will be your delay for that particular encounter.
When RNG abusing in unstable conditions you should record delays for multiple attempts before aiming for a target and check for any consistent delay counts. It is also advised to aim for large clusters where possible to increase your chances of actually hitting the target.

Cannot find result...
If you cannot find the Pokémon you are in battle with anywhere on the tool this is likely due to one of two reasons:
  • The encounter spawned too late and you missed your target frame as a result.
  • The information entered into the main window of the tool was not completely accurate.
Try to reposition your character and see if your issue persists, if you are still having problems double check the information on the main tab is entered correctly by searching it on the Encounter Lookup and clicking the correct row.

You must pay attention to the row you are clicking and making sure it matches your in-game environment.
