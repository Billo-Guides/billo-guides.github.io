Sword/Shield Hidden RNG

If you made it this far, it must mean you have an interest in RNG abusing a Pokemon from a hidden spawn! This page will cover the whole process, from finding a desirable location to achieving a final result.

Getting Started:

Before starting, do the following:
  • Find an ideal location and weather condition for your Pokemon using the SWSH Encounter-slots finder
  • Head to the location in-game and stand near the grass, ideally with minimal NPCs or spawned Pokemon present.
  • Press the Home button on your controller, click connect on the GUI, then fill in the details of your hunt on the GUI accordingly.

Time to get to work!

We will be aiming for a Sinistea-Antique in Glimwood Tangle, and our goal will be to walk into the grass to spawn a hidden encounter on the calculated target frame given to us by the RNG tool GUI. We will be using the advancement methods listed on the main page of this guide, primarily day skip, bike and player movement advances.

The species of a hidden encounter is determined by randomness, which means we need to work out the encounter slot value of our desired Pokemon. Using Sinistea-Antique as the example we can notice an encounter slot range of 99-99, a level range of 34-36 and is a hidden encounter in Glimwood Tangle. Lets enter this information into the GUI!

Once we have our RNG GUI filled in correctly and stood in position in-game, we can begin looking for our target Pokemon! In this example we'll be keeping it simple and aiming for any shiny Sinistea-Antique, you can be more specific with filters if you wish, such as filtering for marks and specific IVs.

If your results are not desirable, repeat the following:
  1. Disconnect your Switch from the GUI.
  2. Restart your game and enter the overworld.
  3. Enter the pause or Home menu and reconnect your Switch to the GUI.
  4. Search again, do this until you have desirable results.
We found one! A shiny, Gentle natured Sinistea-Antique in at 19,139 and 19,141 advances. These are our target frames and we will aim to spawn the encounter on one of these two frames to encounter the Sinistea we are targeting.

It's a good idea to aim for large clusters of shiny frames when you put this guide into practice. The more duplicates in the cluster the more chances we will have at hitting our target Pokemon.

Troubleshooting Encounters

This section covers how to troubleshoot encounters and delays. If you miss a target, spawning in the wrong Pokemon, or can't find your result on the GUI, this is the place to be.

Missing Targets:

The commonly occuring reasons for missing a target Pokemon are:
  • Encounter did not spawn.
  • Encounter spawned too late due to overstepping.
  • There is a delay left unaccounted for.
  • Frame was "hit" but incorrect species of Pokemon was encountered.
Encounter did not spawn or spawned too late...
This is a common issue players will face when tackling hidden encounters. Simply retry and search for a new target frame and make sure your player positioning is correct. Standing directly next to the grass is advised as you will be able to spawn a hidden encounter in 1-2 steps. You will know if you are standing too close when the player starts the crouching animation, this is something we want to avoid as it messes with player movement.

Troubleshooting delay...
Most hidden encounters do not need to account for delay, this mostly applies to encounters in extreme weather conditions or in an otherwise unstable environment, like having other spawned Pokemon present. Your delay is calculated by frame hit - Frame initated = delay. We can use the Read Encounter feature of the GUI to confirm the frame we are hitting and see if we need to account for any delay. Here'x how it's done!
  • Aim for a target, such as any shiny Pokemon.
  • Walk into the grass and initiate an encounter. Take note of the frame you decide to walk into the grass on.
    • Read Encounter button if you didn't find the Pokemon what you expected.
  • Do a filterless search and find the frame you hit, take note of the number and subtract the target frame number from it.
  • This will be your delay for that particular encounter.
If you are RNG abusing in unstable conditions you should record delays for multiple attempts before aiming for a target and check for any consistent delay counts. It is also advised to aim for large clusters where possible to increase your chances of actually hitting the target.

We hit a shiny, but it wasn't Sinistea-Antique! So what happened here? We actually hit a different frame within a shiny cluster which contained the encounterslot values for Morgrem, let's take a look by setting the encounter slot range to 0-99.

Here we can see the the shiny cluster, and Morgrem was indeed a shiny Pokemon within the cluster (Encounterslot value of 0-19). We ended up 2 frames earlier than desired, and will adjust accordingly for next time by entering the grass 2 frames later.

Cannot find result...
If you cannot find your result on the tool this is likely because the Pokemon you encountered does or does not have a random held item and the wrong box is currently checked on the GUI. Click the rand held item box and search again to find the frame you actually hit.
