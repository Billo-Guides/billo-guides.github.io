Sword/Shield Symbol RNG

If you made it this far, it must mean you have an interest in RNG abusing Pokémon from a symbol encounter! This page will cover the whole process, from finding a desirable location to achieving a final result.

Getting Started:

Before starting, do the following:
  • Find an ideal location and weather condition for your Pokémon using the SWSH Encounter-slots finder.
  • Head to the location in-game and stand near the grass, ideally with minimal NPCs or spawned Pokemon present.
  • Run far enough away to despawn the Pokémon in the grass and save.
  • Press the Home button on your controller, click connect on the GUI, then fill in the details of your hunt on the GUI accordingly.

Important Information:

Fill in all the appropriate fields & checkboxes for your hunt...
Rand Held Item - This checkbox should be checked if your Pokémon can hold an item when found in a wild encounter.
Possible EMs - This field should be correctly filled as per the species you are hunting to ensure accurate predictions from the RNG tool.
Weather - This checkbox should be checked if the weather in your location is anything other than sunny.
Fishing - This checkbox should be checked if your Pokémon is found by fishing with a rod.
Cute Charm Lead - This checkbox should be checked if the first Pokémon in your party has the ability Cute Charm.

You can quickly information about held items and egg move count using LegoFigure11's SWSH OWRNG encounter details tool.

Brilliant Aura...
This is the yellow glow occasionally present on symbol and fishing encounters. Brilliant Aura Pokémon are guaranteed to have 2-3 full 31 IVs, this makes them ideal for those who wish to RNG abuse high IV wild Pokémon.

The KO mechanic...
This is a mechanic used to increase the odds of Brilliant Aura and shiny odds of Pokémon with Brilliant Aura. You can find the total number of Pokémon KO'd by looking it up in your Pokédex. This information should be entered into the RNG tool.

It's advised to KO at least 100 of the species you wish to hunt to significantly increase the chances of finding that Pokémon with Brilliant Aura. This Twitter thread by Anubis contains a more detailed break down of odds and statistics.

Time to get to work!

We will be aiming for a Swablu in Giant's Bed, our goal will be to walk towards the grass to spawn a symbol encounter on the calculated target frame given to us by the RNG tool. We will be using the advancement methods listed on the main page of this guide, primarily day skip, bike and player movement advances. The species of symbol encounters is determined by randomness, which means we need to work out the encounter slot value of our desired Pokémon.

Using Swablu as the example;
- The SWSH Encounter-slots finder tells us that Swablu has an encounter slot range of 80-94, a level range of 60-65 and is a symbol encounter in Overcast weather.
- The SWSH OWRNG encounter details tool tells us that Swablu does not hold a random held item and has a number of egg moves.
- Our Pokédex tells us we have >500 KOs on Swablu.

Troubleshooting Encounters

This section covers how to troubleshoot encounters and delays. If you keep missing targets, spawning in the wrong Pokémon, or can't find your result on the RNG tool, this is the place to be.

Missing Targets:

The commonly occuring reasons for missing a target Pokémon are:
  • Encounter did not spawn.
  • Encounter spawned too late due to mistiming.
  • There is a delay left unaccounted for.
  • Frame was "hit" but incorrect species of Pokémon was encountered.
  • Information in the RNG tool is not completely accurate.
Encounter did not spawn or spawned too late...
This is a common issue players will face when tackling symbol encounters. Simply retry and search for a new target frame and make sure your player positioning is correct.

Troubleshooting delay...
Some symbol encounters do not need to account for delay, this mostly applies to encounters in extreme weather conditions or in an otherwise unstable environment, like having other spawned Pokémon present. Your delay is calculated by frame hit - Frame initated = delay. We can use the Read Encounter feature of the GUI to confirm the frame we are hitting and see if we need to account for any delay. Here's how it's done!
  • Aim for a target, such as any shiny Pokémon.
  • Walk towards the grass, spawn the Pokemon and initiate an encounter. Take note of the frame you the Pokémon was spawned on.
    • Read Encounter button if you didn't find the Pokémon what you expected.
  • Do a filterless search and find the frame you hit, take note of the number and subtract the target frame number from it.
  • This will be your delay for that particular encounter.
When RNG abusing in unstable conditions you should record delays for multiple attempts before aiming for a target and check for any consistent delay counts. It is also advised to aim for large clusters where possible to increase your chances of actually hitting the target.

Cannot find result...
If you cannot find the Pokémon you are in battle with anywhere on the tool this is likely due to one of two reasons:
  • Player was positioned too close to the grass and the Pokémon had already been generated long before walking into it.
  • Information entered into the RNG tool was not completely accurate.
Try to reposition your character and see if your issue persists, if you are still having problems double check the information on the tool is entered correctly using the resources found at the top of this page.

Pokémon of different species will potentially have differing egg move count and Pokedex KO count, they also may/may not hold a random held item when found in the wild. These are all contributing factors that need accounting for when troubleshooting your encounters.
