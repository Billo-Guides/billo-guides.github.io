Sword/Shield Static RNG

If you made it this far, it must mean you have an interest in RNG abusing Pokémon from a static spawn! This page will cover the whole process, from finding a desirable location to achieving a final result.

Getting Started:

Before starting, do the following:
  • Find an ideal location and weather condition for your Pokémon using the Encounter Lookup tab of the RNG tool.
  • Click on the row you prefer, this will enter all the information you need into the Seed Finder tab automatically.
  • Head to the location in-game and stand near the spawn point of the Pokémon, ideally with minimal NPCs or other spawned Pokémon present.
  • Run far enough away to despawn the Pokémon and save.
  • Press the Home button on your controller, click Connect on the Seed Finder tab of the tool.

Time to get to work!

The goal is to walk towards our encounter and spawn it on the calculated target frame given by the generator. The advancement methods used are listed on the main page of this guide, day skip, bike and player movement advances will be used for this example.

If you plan to use the DaySkip feature you should take note of how many frames advance per day skipped to make sure you don't overshoot your target.

Static encounters are guaranteed to be the same species every time until the weather in the area changes, weather changes on each new day. This means the encounter will be guaranteed if the correct weather is present!

We will be aiming for a Shiny, Marked Duraludon in Giant's Seat. You can be more specific with the filters if you wish, such as filtering for a specific mark or specific IVs.

If your results are not desirable, repeat the following:
  1. Disconnect your Switch from the tool.
  2. Restart your game and enter the overworld.
  3. Enter the pause or Home menu and reconnect your Switch to the tool.
  4. Search again, do this until you have desirable results.

Different areas & environments can have a drastic influence on delay, if you are missing targets when you believe you should be finding results, you should consider reading the Troubleshooting Encounters section down below.

Troubleshooting Encounters

This section covers how to troubleshoot encounters and delays. If you keep missing targets, spawning in the wrong Pokémon, or can't find your result on the RNG tool, this is the place to be.

Missing Targets:

The commonly occuring reasons for missing a target Pokémon are:
  • Encounter did not spawn.
  • Encounter spawned too late due to mistiming.
  • There is a delay left unaccounted for.
  • Information in the Seed Finder tab was not completely accurate.
Encounter did not spawn or spawned too late...
This is a common issue players will face when tackling static encounters. Simply retry and search for a new target frame and make sure your player positioning is correct.

Troubleshooting delay...
Most static encounters do not need to account for delay, this applies most to encounters in extreme weather conditions or in an otherwise unstable environment, like having other spawned Pokémon present. Your delay is calculated by frame hit - Frame initated = delay. We can use the Read Encounter feature of the tool to confirm the frame we are hitting and see if we need to account for any delay. Here's how it's done!
  • Aim for a target, such as any shiny Pokémon.
  • Walk towards the grass, spawn the Pokemon and initiate an encounter. Take note of the frame you the Pokémon was spawned on.
  • Encounter the Pokemon and click the Read Encounter button if you didn't find the Pokémon what you expected.
  • Do a filterless search and find the frame you hit, take note of the number and subtract the target frame number from it.
  • This will be your delay for that particular encounter.
When RNG abusing in unstable conditions you should record delays for multiple attempts before aiming for a target and check for any consistent delay counts. It is also advised to aim for large clusters where possible to increase your chances of actually hitting the target.

Cannot find result...
If you cannot find the Pokémon you are in battle with anywhere on the tool this is likely due to one of two reasons:
  • Player was positioned too close to the spawn and the Pokémon had already been generated long before walking into it.
  • The information entered into the Seed Finder tab of the tool was not completely accurate.
Try to reposition your character and see if your issue persists, if you are still having problems double check the information on the Seed Finder tab is entered correctly by searching it on the Encounter Lookup and clicking the correct row. You must pay attention to the row you are clicking and making sure it matches your in-game environment.
