Sword/Shield Static RNG

If you made it this far, it must mean you have an interest in RNG abusing Pokémon from a static spawn! This page will cover the whole process, from finding a desirable location to achieving a final result.

Getting Started:

Before starting, do the following:
  • Find an ideal location and weather condition for your Pokémon using the SWSH Encounter-slots finder.
  • Head to the location in-game and stand near the grass, ideally with minimal NPCs or spawned Pokemon present.
  • Run far enough away to despawn the Pokémon in the grass and save.
  • Press the Home button on your controller, click Connect on the RNG tool, then fill in the details of your hunt on the tool accordingly.

Important Information:

Fill in all the appropriate fields & checkboxes for your hunt...
Static - This checkbox should be checked if your Pokémon is from a static encounter.
Weather - This checkbox should be checked if the weather in your location is anything other than sunny.
Locked Ability - This checkbox should be checked for any Pokémon with a fixed ability, such as the Swords of Justice and the Galarian Bird Trio.
Shiny Locked - This checkbox should be checked if the Pokémon is shiny locked, like Keldeo and the Galarian Bird Trio
Flawless IVs - This field should be filled in correctly if the Pokémon has any guaranteed full 31 IVs, most notably all legendary spawns and Spiritomb.

Time to get to work!

We will be aiming for a Aerodactyl in Giant's Bed, our goal will be to walk towards the grass to spawn our encounter on the calculated target frame given to us by the RNG tool. We will be using the advancement methods listed on the main page of this guide, primarily day skip, bike and player movement advances.

Static encounters are guaranteed to be the same species every time until the weather in the area changes, weather changes on each new day. Which means we do not need to worry about the encounter slot value of our desired Pokémon and that we can get straight to work once we have found our location!

Troubleshooting Encounters

This section covers how to troubleshoot encounters and delays. If you keep missing targets, spawning in the wrong Pokémon, or can't find your result on the RNG tool, this is the place to be.

Missing Targets:

The commonly occuring reasons for missing a target Pokémon are:
  • Encounter did not spawn.
  • Encounter spawned too late due to mistiming.
  • There is a delay left unaccounted for.
  • Information in the RNG tool is not completely accurate.
Encounter did not spawn or spawned too late...
This is a common issue players will face when tackling static encounters. Simply retry and search for a new target frame and make sure your player positioning is correct.

Troubleshooting delay...
Most static encounters do not need to account for delay, this mostly applies to encounters in extreme weather conditions or in an otherwise unstable environment, like having other spawned Pokémon present. Your delay is calculated by frame hit - Frame initated = delay. We can use the Read Encounter feature of the RNG tool to confirm the frame we are hitting and see if we need to account for any delay. Here's how it's done!
  • Aim for a target, such as any shiny Pokémon.
  • Walk towards the grass, spawn the Pokemon and initiate an encounter. Take note of the frame you the Pokémon was spawned on.
    • Read Encounter button if you didn't find the Pokémon what you expected.
  • Do a filterless search and find the frame you hit, take note of the number and subtract the target frame number from it.
  • This will be your delay for that particular encounter.
When RNG abusing in unstable conditions you should record delays for multiple attempts before aiming for a target and check for any consistent delay counts. It is also advised to aim for large clusters where possible to increase your chances of actually hitting the target.

Cannot find result...
If you cannot find the Pokémon you are in battle with anywhere on the tool this is likely due to one of two reasons:
  • Player was positioned too close to the grass and the Pokémon had already been generated long before walking into it.
  • Information entered into the RNG tool was not completely accurate.
Try to reposition your character and see if your issue persists, if you are still having problems double check the information on the tool is entered correctly using the resources found at the top of this page.

Ensure that the box for static encounters is checked, if there is active weather in the area you are in then ensure the weather box is checked too.
